# Sox9 Is Required for Nail-Bed Differentiation and Digit-Tip Regeneration

## Citation (NLM)
Lao M, Hurtado A, Chacón de Castro A, Burgos M, Jiménez R, Barrionuevo FJ. Sox9 Is Required for Nail-Bed Differentiation and Digit-Tip Regeneration. J Invest Dermatol. 2022;142(10):2613-2622. doi:10.1016/j.jid.2022.03.020

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jid.2022.03.020](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jid.2022.03.020)

---

## Background

The mammalian nail organ is a specialized appendage in which multiple ectodermal tissues (nail matrix, nail bed, eponychium, hyponychium) coordinately sustain continuous nail growth. Two nail stem cell pools are known: slow-cycling label-retaining cells around the nail root, and highly proliferative self-renewing stem cells in the nail matrix that feed both the nail plate and nail bed (with Wnt/β-catenin activated in the distal matrix). Importantly, nail growth is coupled to digit-tip regeneration — mouse and infant human digit tips can fully regenerate after amputation via wound healing, re-epithelialisation, and formation of a blastema (a mesenchymal progenitor mass) beneath the wound. The nail organ signals the underlying blastema, and Wnt effectors such as β-catenin and LGR6 are required for digit-tip regeneration.

The transcription factor SOX9 has multiple roles in development and adult tissue homeostasis. This study characterizes Sox9 expression in the mouse digit tip and, using lineage tracing and conditional knockout, tests its function in nail-bed differentiation, terminal phalanx maintenance, and digit-tip regeneration.

---

## Key Experiment Methods

1. Immunofluorescence for SOX9 with epidermal markers K5 (keratin 5), TCF1 (Wnt effector), and Ki-67 (proliferation) to map SOX9+ populations in the digit tip.
2. Tamoxifen-inducible genetic lineage tracing using *Sox9^IRES-CreERT2* crossed to *R26R-LacZ* or *R26R-EYFP* reporters; reporter expression analyzed over time (days after tamoxifen administration, DATX), including 1-year chase.
3. Conditional Sox9 deletion in adult mice with ubiquitous tamoxifen-inducible *CAGG-CreERTM;Sox9^flox/flox* (Sox9^Δ/Δ); gross morphology, histology, and time-course marker analysis (K5, TCF1, Ki-67, AE13 keratinized-plate marker).
4. Terminal phalanx assessment: whole-mount alizarin red (bone), TUNEL (apoptosis/DNA fragmentation), and tartrate-resistant acid phosphatase (TRAP, osteoclast) staining.
5. Digit-tip amputation with tamoxifen from the time of amputation; alizarin red bone measurement at 50 days after amputation (DAA), plus blastema analysis by TCF1, TUNEL, AE13, and RUNX2 (osteoblast commitment) immunostaining.

---

## Results

- SOX9 is expressed in multiple digit-tip populations (hair follicles, distal groove, proximal nail fold, hyponychium basal layers) and, within the nail epithelium, specifically in the non-proliferative nail bed (distal to the Ki-67+ proximal/distal matrix and overlapping the distal end of the TCF1 domain).
- Lineage tracing showed nail-bed epithelial cells derive from Sox9-expressing cells as a transient population: labeling increased from 1→10 DATX (not by proliferation, since nail-bed cells do not divide) then was progressively lost proximal-to-distal by 40–45 DATX. A separate Sox9+ progenitor population in basal layers continuously supplies the entire hyponychium epidermis (EYFP+ basal and corneocyte cells at 40 DATX; LacZ persisting 1 year).
- Sox9^Δ/Δ mice developed outlandishly large, claw-shaped fingernails: the hyponychium was absent, the nail bed failed to form, and the entire nail epithelium became thick and matrix-like — expanded K5, distally extended TCF1, and markedly increased Ki-67, with intense TUNEL+ keratinization (AE13+). Nail matrix stem cells thus fail to differentiate into nail-bed cells and instead proliferate and follow the corneocyte/nail-plate fate.
- The underlying terminal phalanx underwent bone regression: shorter, blunt/pitted tips by 40–50 DATX, with apoptotic (TUNEL+) cells and active osteoclasts (TRAP+) at the distal tip.
- Digit-tip regeneration was compromised in Sox9^Δ/Δ mice: after amputation, bones remained significantly shorter and lacked the distal tip, the nail matrix domain (TCF1) extended distally, keratinization increased, and RUNX2+ osteoblast commitment in the blastema was reduced by half — indicating impaired bone regeneration.

---

## Perspective

This study identifies Sox9 as essential for epithelial nail-bed differentiation, for maintenance of the terminal phalanx, and for digit-tip regeneration in mice. Mechanistically, Sox9 restrains nail matrix stem cell fate: without it, cells over-proliferate and default to the cornified nail-plate program, the nail bed and hyponychium are lost, and blastema osteoblast commitment (RUNX2) fails, blunting bone regeneration. It defines a Sox9+ transient nail-bed lineage and a continuous Sox9+ hyponychium supply.

For regenerative biology, the work is relevant as a mammalian blastema-based (digit-tip) regeneration model, linking an epithelial transcription factor to mesenchymal blastema osteogenesis via nail-organ signaling. Limitations include the use of a ubiquitous conditional knockout (effects may not be entirely nail-epithelium-autonomous) and reliance on marker/histological readouts rather than direct molecular dissection of the Sox9-to-blastema signaling axis. The findings inform understanding of nail organ homeostasis/disease and could aid strategies for nail disorders and digit amputation.
