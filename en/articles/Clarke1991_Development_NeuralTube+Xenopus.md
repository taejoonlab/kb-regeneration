# Neuroanatomical and functional analysis of neural tube formation in notochordless Xenopus embryos; laterality of the ventral spinal cord is lost

## Citation (NLM)
Clarke JDW, Holder N, Soffe SR, Storm-Mathisen J. Neuroanatomical and functional analysis of neural tube formation in notochordless Xenopus embryos; laterality of the ventral spinal cord is lost. Development. 1991;112(2):499-516.

---

## Background
The vertebrate neural tube is patterned in part by inductive interactions between mesoderm and ectoderm. One key interaction is thought to be induction of the floor plate — a specialized group of ventral midline neuroepithelial cells — by the underlying notochord. Prior evidence in chick showed that ectopic notochord grafts induce ectopic floor plates and that notochord removal yields floorplateless neural tubes. The floor plate had been implicated in dorsoventral patterning of the neural tube and in guiding commissural axons across the midline.

This study examines the neuroanatomical and functional consequences of neural tube development in notochordless *Xenopus laevis* embryos, produced by UV irradiation of the vegetal pole of the fertilized uncleaved egg (which disrupts axial mesoderm in a dose-dependent manner). The authors assess whether the floor plate is missing, how sensory/motor neuron populations are altered, how commissural axons behave, and whether locomotor laterality is preserved.

---

## Key Experiment Methods
1. **Notochordless embryo production:** UV irradiation (254 nm, 3 min) of the vegetal pole of fertilized uncleaved *Xenopus* eggs; embryos graded by index of axial deficiency (IAD); mostly grade 1-2 used.
2. **Wax histology and electron microscopy:** Serial transverse sections (H&E; methylene blue/lead citrate/uranyl acetate) to assess cord structure and floor plate morphology.
3. **Retrograde HRP labeling:** HRP applied to myotomes, rostral/caudal cord, or hindbrain to reveal neuronal classes (motor neurons, Rohon-Beard cells, extramedullary cells, commissural/dorsolateral commissural interneurons, Kolmer-Agduhr cells, ascending/descending interneurons) and their morphology/position/number.
4. **Anti-vimentin (Z9) immunohistochemistry:** To label radial glial processes and distinguish floor plate cells (normally vimentin-negative).
5. **Anti-glycine immunohistochemistry:** To selectively reveal glycinergic commissural neurons and measure axon crossing angles/directions.
6. **Extracellular electrophysiology:** Suction electrodes at intermyotomal clefts recorded fictive locomotor (ventral root) activity in curarized embryos to assess left/right alternation.

---

## Results
- **Floor plate is absent:** No cuboidal floor plate cells are present ventral to the neurocoel; the ventral midline is instead occupied by motor neurons, Kolmer-Agduhr cells, radial glia and a single ventral marginal zone. Vimentin-positive radial processes (normally confined to lateral marginal zones) extend across the ventral midline, and the neurocoel is displaced dorsally.
- **Motor neurons reduced to ~15% of control;** the loss is predominantly of the smallest cells, suggesting that later-born secondary motor neurons are missing and mainly primary motor neurons remain. Motor neuron cell bodies are misplaced midventrally, with dendrites extending into both left and right marginal zones (loss of laterality).
- **Extramedullary (sensory) cells increased ~20-fold;** these may be misplaced Rohon-Beard cells that migrated out through incomplete neural tube closure / a defective glia limitans (Rohon-Beard cell number within the cord unchanged).
- **Commissural axons still cross** the ventral cord but at abnormal, more variable and often shallow angles; a larger proportion grow caudally, and some fail to turn longitudinally, continuing circumferentially to the dorsal surface. Glycinergic axon crossing angle variability increased markedly (in normals 66% within 10° of 90°; in experimentals only 20%).
- **Loss of functional laterality:** Notochordless embryos cannot swim normally. Fictive locomotor output is rhythmic but synchronous on both sides rather than alternating — resembling a surgically isolated single side of the cord.
- Rescue of the dorsal marginal zone in prior work restores normal axial structures, supporting that the defects arise from disrupted notochord-neural tube interaction rather than another UV mechanism.

---

## Perspective
The study provides functional and anatomical evidence that the notochord (and/or the floor plate it induces) is required to impose left/right laterality on the ventral spinal cord — governing motor neuron dendrite confinement, separation of left/right marginal zones, and the reciprocal glycinergic inhibitory circuitry that drives alternating swimming. It also supports a role for the notochord/floor plate in dorsoventral patterning (motor neuron production, restraining dorsal sensory cell numbers), though the persistence of primary motor neurons argues against a simple "all cells dorsalized" morphogen model and for an inductive requirement for motor neuron generation. Limitations include the absence of a *Xenopus*-specific floor plate biochemical marker (floor plate absence inferred morphologically), possible incomplete UV effects, and unresolved premotor circuit connectivity. The authors propose the floor plate may act as a less-attractive growth substrate imposing laterality, and note ongoing work on Kolmer-Agduhr/GABAergic scaffolds guiding commissural axons.
