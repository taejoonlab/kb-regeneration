# Placenta to cartilage: direct conversion of human placenta to chondrocytes with transformation by defined factors

## Citation (NLM)
Ishii R, Kami D, Toyoda M, Makino H, Gojo S, Ishii T, Umezawa A. Placenta to cartilage: direct conversion of human placenta to chondrocytes with transformation by defined factors. *Mol Biol Cell*. 2012 Sep 15;23(18):3511-21. doi:10.1091/mbc.E11-10-0869

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1091/mbc.E11-10-0869](https://doi.org/10.1091/mbc.E11-10-0869)

---

## Background

Direct conversion (direct reprogramming) is a technique that converts one differentiated cell type into another without passing through an intermediate dedifferentiated state. This approach has been successfully reported for β-cells, cardiomyocytes, and neurons. For cartilage regeneration, the ability to directly convert human somatic cells into chondrocytes has significant implications for cell therapy and tissue engineering. The placental membrane, a discarded resource after childbirth, is an easily obtainable cell source without ethical concerns. This study aimed to directly convert human placenta-derived cells (smooth chorion and decidua) into chondrocytes by introducing specific combinations of transcription factors.

---

## Key Experiment Methods

1. **Cell isolation and culture**: After removing the amniotic membrane from placental membranes, smooth chorion and decidua tissues were cut into 2 mm³ pieces and cells were isolated and expanded by explant culture. Parental cells at 4 population doublings (PD) were used for conversion experiments.

2. **Retroviral vector construction and infection**: Starting from a pool of 14 genes, a combination of 5 transcription factors (BCL6, T/BRACHYURY, c-MYC, MITF, BAF60C/SMARCD3) was selected for inducing chondrocyte-specific gene expression (COL2A1, COL10A1, CRTL1, ACAN). Each gene was cloned into the pMXs vector and retroviruses were produced in 293FT cells. Parental cells were pretreated with p53 siRNA prior to infection with the 5 retrovirus mixture. Cells were cultured on MEF feeders and colony formation was monitored.

3. **Gene expression analysis**: Chondrocyte-specific gene expression (SOX5, SOX6, SOX9, COL2A1, COL1A1, COL10A1, CRTL1, ACAN, TERT) was evaluated by RT-PCR and quantitative RT-PCR. Endogenous T, MITF, and BAF60C expression was confirmed using primers non-specific to transgenes.

4. **Flow cytometry (FACS)**: Surface marker expression of CD44, CD49c, CD151, CD166, CD117, and CD133 was analyzed.

5. **Gene expression microarray**: Agilent 4×44K whole human genome microarrays were performed. Transcriptome similarity was assessed by hierarchical clustering and principal component analysis (PCA).

6. **siRNA inhibition experiments**: iCS cells were transfected with siRNAs targeting each of the 5 genes (BCL6, T, c-MYC, MITF, BAF60C) and changes in chondrocyte-specific gene expression were analyzed.

7. **In vivo cartilage formation assay**: iCS cells were injected subcutaneously into immunodeficient Balb/c nu/nu mice. After 7 weeks, tumors were harvested and analyzed by H&E, Alcian blue, and toluidine blue staining, as well as immunohistochemistry (vimentin, collagen type II, Ki-67).

8. **Colony formation assay**: Cells were cultured in MethoCult H4034 medium for 14 days and colonies were counted.

9. **Karyotype and genetic verification**: G-band karyotyping, Southern blot, short tandem repeat (STR) analysis, and genomic DNA PCR were performed to confirm gene integration and cell origin.

---

## Results

**Selection of 5 transcription factor combination**: Starting from a pool of 14 mesoderm and chromatin remodeling genes, a 5-gene combination (BCL6, T, c-MYC, MITF, BAF60C) most efficiently induced chondrocyte-specific gene expression. Removal of any single factor significantly reduced gene induction.

**Establishment of iCS cell lines**: By 15 days after 5F pool infection, numerous colonies formed on MEF feeders (efficiency 5.76 × 10⁻⁴), and 4 clones proliferated for >150 days while expressing TERT. Each clone maintained a normal karyotype (46XX or 46XY), and STR analysis confirmed their origin from parental cells.

**In vitro chondrocyte phenotype**: All iCS clones expressed chondrocyte-specific genes and proteins including SOX5/6/9, COL2A1, COL1A1, CRTL1, and ACAN by RT-PCR and Western blot. FACS analysis showed CD44⁺, CD49c⁺, CD151⁺, CD166⁺, CD117⁻, and CD133⁻, consistent with the chondrocyte surface marker pattern. Microarray analysis revealed that iCS cells displayed transcriptome profiles similar to human adult and fetal chondrocytes (hierarchical clustering and PCA).

**Endogenous gene reprogramming**: Expression of endogenous T, MITF, and BAF60C genes was induced after conversion, suggesting cellular reprogramming beyond mere transgene overexpression.

**siRNA functional validation**: T gene siRNA treatment significantly reduced chondrogenic genes including SOX5, SOX6, COL1A1, and COL2A1; similar effects were observed with MITF siRNA, demonstrating that T and MITF are essential for chondrocyte conversion. BCL6, c-MYC, and BAF60C siRNAs did not substantially affect chondrogenic gene expression.

**In vivo cartilage formation**: Seven weeks after subcutaneous injection of iCS cells into mice, toluidine blue metachromasia and Alcian blue-positive cartilage tissue were observed. Human-specific vimentin antibody confirmed human cell-derived cartilage, and collagen type II immunostaining was positive. Ki-67 positivity (~30%) indicated sustained proliferative activity. The tissue was histopathologically diagnosed as chondrosarcoma.

**Conversion from diverse cell sources**: Similar iCS cell lines were established from menstrual blood-derived cells and placental artery endothelial cells, demonstrating the versatility of this chondrocyte conversion technology.

---

## Perspective

This study is the first to successfully convert human non-cartilage tissue (placenta) directly into chondrocytes using a combination of 5 transcription factors (BCL6, T, c-MYC, MITF, BAF60C). Notably, chondrocyte conversion was achieved without SOX9, and T (brachyury) and MITF were identified as critical determinants of chondrocyte fate. T is a T-box transcription factor involved in mesoderm formation and chondrogenesis, confirmed by siRNA experiments to be essential for cartilage conversion.

Interestingly, unlike the previous mouse fibroblast study (using Sox9, c-Myc, Klf4), Klf4 was dispensable for human placental cells, reflecting differences in cell source and species. BAF60C, a chromatin remodeling factor, may act as an epigenetic regulator that initiates chondrogenic gene expression, analogous to its role in allowing Gata4 DNA accessibility during cardiomyocyte conversion.

A significant limitation of this study is that iCS cells simultaneously acquired characteristics of chondrosarcoma (malignant transformation). Retroviral integration, c-MYC expression, and p53 siRNA treatment likely contributed to this transformation, representing a safety issue that must be resolved before clinical application. Additionally, in terms of global gene expression, iCS cells are not completely identical to normal chondrocytes.

Future challenges include developing non-transformed chondrocyte conversion methods, utilizing iCS cells for scaffold screening in cartilage tissue engineering, and performing long-term safety evaluations. This technology, leveraging the abundant and accessible cell source of the placenta, represents an important advance for cartilage regenerative medicine and chondrogenesis research.
