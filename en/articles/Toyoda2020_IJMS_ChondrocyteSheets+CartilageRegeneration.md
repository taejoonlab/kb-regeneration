# Transcriptomic and Proteomic Analyses Reveal the Potential Mode of Action of Chondrocyte Sheets in Hyaline Cartilage Regeneration

## Citation (NLM)
Toyoda E, Sato M, Takahashi T, Maehara M, Okada E, Wasai S, Iijima H, Nonaka K, Kawaguchi Y, Watanabe M. Transcriptomic and Proteomic Analyses Reveal the Potential Mode of Action of Chondrocyte Sheets in Hyaline Cartilage Regeneration. Int J Mol Sci. 2020;21(1):149. doi:10.3390/ijms21010149

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.3390/ijms21010149](https://doi.org/10.3390/ijms21010149)

---

## Background
Articular cartilage has limited self-repair capacity, and osteoarthritis (OA) affects approximately half of those over 40 years old. Chondrocyte sheet transplantation is a promising approach for treating cartilage defects, with clinical research demonstrating hyaline cartilage regeneration using autologous cells. Polydactyly-derived chondrocyte sheets (PD sheets) offer an allogeneic alternative with excellent proliferative capacity. However, the characteristics of PD sheets vary depending on donor differences, culture conditions, and passage numbers, necessitating identification of biological markers to predict their in vivo efficacy for cartilage regeneration.

## Key Experiment Methods
1. Fabrication of 12 lots of PD sheets from chondrocytes obtained from 10 juvenile polydactyly donors, with varying passage numbers and culture conditions
2. Orthotopic xenogeneic transplantation into immunosuppressed rabbit osteochondral defect models (5 mm diameter, 3 mm depth) with histological scoring using ICRS criteria at 4 weeks
3. Microarray gene expression analysis of PD sheets to identify efficacy-correlated genes
4. Proteomic analysis using SOMAscan aptamer-based platform detecting 1129 proteins in PD sheet supernatants
5. Enrichment analysis using Metascape for functional annotation of efficacy-related genes and proteins
6. RT-PCR validation of candidate marker genes (ACAN, ITGA10, GDF5, SOX9, SOX5) and calculation of gene expression score (GES)
7. ELISA quantification of secreted proteins (MIA, DKK1, GREM1, NTN4, ESM1) and correlation with ICRS scores
8. Flow cytometric analysis of surface markers (CD31, CD45, CD81, CD90, CD44) and histological/immunohistochemical staining (COL1, COL2, vimentin)

## Results
- PD sheet transplantation resulted in ICRS scores ranging from 18.1 to 35.2, with 4 of 12 lots showing statistically significant efficacy in repairing osteochondral defects; regenerated tissue contained human vimentin-positive cells confirming donor origin
- Microarray analysis identified 205 positively correlated and 238 negatively correlated genes with ICRS scores; 171 differentially expressed genes (>2-fold change) separated effective from less effective lots
- Enrichment analysis revealed efficacy-correlated genes associated with extracellular matrix organization, cartilage development, skeletal development, angiogenesis, and mesenchymal stem cell differentiation
- Eight genes (ESM1, GREM1, SERPINA3, DKK1, MIA, NTN4, FABP3, PDGFA) showed positive correlation and three genes (RARRES2, APOE, PGF) showed negative correlation in both transcriptomic and proteomic analyses
- RT-PCR validation confirmed correlation of ACAN, ITGA10, GDF5, and SOX9 expression with ICRS scores; calculated GES showed correlation coefficient of 0.44 with ICRS scores
- ELISA confirmed variable secretion levels: DKK1 (2.6-26.6 ng/mL), GREM1 (58.2-138 ng/mL), MIA (0-38.4 ng/mL), NTN4 (153-714 pg/mL), ESM1 (128-849 pg/mL); GREM1 and ESM1 showed moderate correlation with efficacy
- PD sheets expressed MSC markers (CD90, CD44 >95% positive) and were negative for blood lineage markers (CD31, CD45)

## Perspective
This study identifies biological pathways and secreted factors (MIA, GREM1, DKK1, ESM1, NTN4) that contribute to hyaline cartilage regeneration by PD sheet transplantation. These factors may serve as quality markers to predict in vivo efficacy when selecting donors or optimizing culture conditions. GREM1 and DKK1 regulate Wnt signaling and suppress hypertrophic chondrocyte differentiation, while ESM1 and NTN4 may suppress angiogenesis during cartilage repair. Limitations include the use of a xenograft model with immunosuppressants, which may differ from allogeneic transplantation in humans. Further validation in independent datasets and human clinical studies is needed before these markers can be used as definitive quality control criteria for PD sheet products.
