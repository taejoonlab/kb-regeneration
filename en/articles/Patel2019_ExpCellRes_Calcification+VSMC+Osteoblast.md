# Differing calcification processes in cultured vascular smooth muscle cells and osteoblasts

## Citation (NLM)

Patel JJ, Bourne LE, Davies BK, Arnett TR, MacRae VE, Wheeler-Jones CPD, Orriss IR. Differing calcification processes in cultured vascular smooth muscle cells and osteoblasts. Exp Cell Res. 2019;380(1):100-113. doi:10.1016/j.yexcr.2019.04.020

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1016/j.yexcr.2019.04.020](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.yexcr.2019.04.020)

---

## Background

Arterial medial calcification (AMC) — the deposition of calcium-phosphate mineral (often hydroxyapatite) in the tunica media — is a common consequence of ageing, atherosclerosis, diabetes, and chronic kidney disease that stiffens vessels and reduces blood flow. AMC is widely modeled as a phenotypic transdifferentiation of vascular smooth muscle cells (VSMCs) toward an osteoblast-like state, and calcified arteries express bone-associated proteins (OPN, TNAP, Runx2). Mineralization is governed by the local pyrophosphate/phosphate ratio, set by the opposing enzymes NPP1 (generates inhibitory pyrophosphate) and TNAP (breaks it down). However, most in vitro AMC studies examine VSMCs alone with excessive phosphate, and "osteoblast-like" classification often rests on non-specific osteogenic marker mRNAs. This study directly compared, side-by-side, established primary mouse VSMC calcification and calvarial osteoblast bone-formation assays.

---

## Key Experiment Methods

**1. Parallel primary-cell assays**
- VSMCs from aortas of 6–8 week C57BL/6J mice; osteoblasts from calvariae of 3–5 day mice
- VSMC calcifying medium: 2 mM sodium orthophosphate (defined time points day 7/10/14); osteoblast medium: 2 mM β-glycerophosphate (day 4/7/10/14); non-passaged cells throughout
- A "universal" 1 mM β-glycerophosphate + 1 mM sodium diphosphate medium tested to compare both processes under identical conditions

**2. Calcification / bone-formation readouts**
- Colorimetric calcium (o-cresolphthalein) and Alizarin red S staining
- Elastic Stain and immunofluorescence (Col1α1, SM22α); Sircol soluble collagen and Fastin elastin assays
- Transmission electron microscopy of matrix ultrastructure

**3. Enzyme, viability, and marker analysis**
- TNAP activity/staining and TNAP inhibition (CAS496014-13-2, 0.1–10 μM); total NPP activity; ATP release (luciferin-luciferase)
- Cell number/viability (LDH CytoTox 96) and apoptosis (annexin V flow cytometry)
- qRT-PCR and Western blot of osteoblast markers (Runx2, Sp7, Alpl, Bglap2, Spp1/OPN) and VSMC markers (Sm22α, Acta2, Myh10, Cald1, Smtn)

---

## Results

**Distinct calcification morphology**
Osteoblasts reproducibly formed large mineralized bone nodules embedded in widespread collagenous matrix. Calcifying VSMCs instead formed small, discrete regions of calcification not associated with collagen and not resembling bone. Total deposited calcium was similar between bone-forming osteoblasts and calcifying VSMCs (~90% higher than control VSMCs). Notably, β-glycerophosphate did not calcify VSMCs — only sodium orthophosphate did.

**Extracellular matrix differs fundamentally**
Both cell types expressed Col1α1 mRNA/protein, but osteoblasts secreted and deposited abundant soluble/fibrillar collagen (banded on TEM), whereas VSMC Col1α1 was cell-associated with no detectable soluble or deposited collagen. VSMC calcification localized on filamentous cellular debris and smaller extracellular vesicles rather than collagen fibrils/matrix vesicles. Control VSMCs had the highest elastin, which fell with calcification.

**TNAP dependence separates the two processes**
Bone-forming osteoblasts had ~100-fold higher TNAP activity than calcifying VSMCs, and TNAP inhibition reduced bone formation by up to 90% but had no effect on VSMC calcification. Conversely, total NPP activity ranked control VSMCs > calcifying VSMCs > osteoblasts. ATP release was highest in osteoblasts. Thus VSMC calcification does not rely on TNAP-driven pyrophosphate hydrolysis the way osteoblast mineralization does.

**Osteogenic markers up but sub-osteoblastic; viability lost**
Calcifying VSMCs increased Runx2, Sp7, Alpl, Bglap2, and Spp1 relative to control VSMCs, but expression remained up to 40-fold lower than in bone-forming osteoblasts, and Runx2 protein was similar in calcifying and control cells. Calcification progressively reduced VSMC viability (up to 7-fold more dead cells) and nearly doubled apoptosis at day 7, whereas osteoblast and control-VSMC viability was unchanged.

---

## Perspective

This study argues that in vitro VSMC calcification is a process distinct from osteoblast bone-nodule formation, not a faithful recapitulation of osteogenesis. Calcifying VSMCs occupy a transitional phenotype between a normal VSMC and a mature osteoblast: they acquire some osteoblast markers but fail to build collagen-containing bone, do not depend on TNAP for mineral deposition, and — unlike osteoblasts — lose viability as they calcify.

For the vault's ossification theme, the paper is an important methodological corrective. It shows that "osteoblast-like" transdifferentiation inferred from osteogenic-marker mRNA overstates the resemblance: the up-regulated Runx2/Sp7/Alpl/Bglap2 program in VSMCs is quantitatively far below true osteoblast levels and is not accompanied by the collagenous, TNAP-dependent matrix that defines bone. This directly informs how ectopic-calcification and vascular-calcification models elsewhere in the vault should be interpreted.

The authors also introduce a "universal" phosphate medium (1 mM β-glycerophosphate + 1 mM diphosphate) that drives both regulated osteoblast bone formation and VSMC calcification under identical conditions, preserving the cell-type differences and enabling cleaner side-by-side comparison. Limitations are inherent to the in vitro cultures and the mouse primary-cell system, which may not capture the in vivo complexity of AMC.
