# Dynamic Compression Improves Chondrogenesis in the Tissue Engineered Model of Cartilage

## Citation (NLM)
Farcasanu MV, de las Heras Ruiz T, Johnson de Sousa Brito FM, Soul J, Coxhead J, German MJ, Young DA, Ferreira-Duarte AM, Piróg KA. Dynamic Compression Improves Chondrogenesis in the Tissue Engineered Model of Cartilage. Biotechnol Bioeng. 2025;122(9):2574-91. doi:10.1002/bit.29026

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1002/bit.29026](https://doi.org/10.1002/bit.29026)

---

## Background
Hyaline cartilage is an avascular tissue with low regenerative capacity, and research on skeletal diseases is constrained by tissue complexity, difficulty in obtaining human samples, and the cost of animal models. Recent advances in tissue engineering have made it possible to develop zonally stratified cartilage models with mechanical responsiveness in vitro. The ATDC5 cell line is widely used for chondrogenesis modeling, but 2D culture fails to recapitulate the zonal stratification of cartilage and tends toward dedifferentiation. This study aimed to optimize a 2% agarose hydrogel 3D culture system under physiological dynamic compression to improve chondrogenesis and to develop an adaptable in vitro cartilage model responsive to mechanical stimulation.

---

## Key Experiment Methods
1. Evaluation of BMP7 (10 ng/mL) and/or TGFβ3 (10 ng/mL) supplementation in ATDC5 chondroprogenitor cell pellet culture
2. ATDC5 cells were encapsulated in 2% low-melting agarose (10×10⁶ cells/mL) and cultured as 3D hydrogel constructs fabricated using metal molds (21 days)
3. Dynamic compression using a Flexcell FX5000™ Pneumatic Compression System: 30 min daily, 10 kPa, 0.33 Hz sinusoidal stimulation (starting from day 7)
4. Pericellular matrix (PCM) observation by SEM, tissue nanohardness (Young's modulus) measurement by AFM
5. Toluidine Blue, Alcian Blue, Picrosirius Red staining and collagen type I/II/X immunohistochemistry
6. Sulfated GAG (sGAG) quantification by DMMB assay, metabolic activity measurement by MTT assay, apoptosis measurement by TUNEL assay
7. Gene expression analysis of chondrogenic (Sox9, Col2a1, Acan), differentiation (Col10a1, Ihh), and dedifferentiation (Col1a1) markers by qRT-PCR
8. RNA sequencing (Illumina NextSeq, 75-bp single read) and GO/KEGG pathway analysis using DAVID

---

## Results
1. Combined BMP7 and TGFβ3 supplementation most effectively enhanced ATDC5 chondrogenesis in both pellet and agarose cultures — markedly increasing Sox9, Col2a1, and Acan expression
2. ATDC5 cells in 2% agarose maintained a rounded chondrocyte morphology and deposited proteoglycans and abundant ECM
3. Agarose encapsulation reduced collagen type I (dedifferentiation marker) expression compared to 2D culture and increased expression of cartilage-specific collagen genes including Col2a1, Col9a1, and Col10a1
4. AFM measurements at day 21 showed tissue nanohardness exceeding 15 kPa, reaching levels comparable to native mouse cartilage
5. Dynamic compression significantly increased chondrogenic marker (Sox9, Col2a1, Acan) expression and decreased Col1a1 (dedifferentiation marker) expression
6. Dynamic compression reduced expression of catabolic markers including Col1a1 (2.1-fold), Il1r1 (5.1-fold), Mmp3 (10.9-fold), and Mmp13 (6.4-fold)
7. GO terms observed exclusively in the compression group included hypoxia response, Notch signaling, chondrocyte differentiation, circadian rhythm, and mechanical stimulus response
8. Dynamic compression increased cell proliferation without affecting metabolic activity, suppressed the early proliferative immature chondrocyte phenotype, and promoted a juvenile cartilage phenotype

---

## Perspective
This study demonstrates that encapsulating ATDC5 cells in 2% agarose hydrogel and applying physiological dynamic compression can markedly improve chondrogenesis. Specifically, combined BMP7 and TGFβ3 supplementation was most effective, and daily 30-minute low-intensity dynamic compression suppressed dedifferentiation and catabolic markers while promoting cartilage-specific gene expression. This model is easy to fabricate and uses the commercially available ATDC5 cell line, offering high reproducibility and scalability, and is well-suited for studying the interplay between mechanical responsiveness, intracellular signaling, and pericellular matrix. The key contribution is the systematic demonstration that chondrogenesis is synergistically enhanced when 3D culture and mechanical stimulation are combined. Limitations include that the ATDC5 cell line may not fully recapitulate primary cells, and that long-term in vivo transplantation studies are necessary. This model may serve as a platform for studying mechanical responses in skeletal diseases and for preclinical screening of therapeutic compounds.
