# Limb Mesoderm and Head Ectomesenchyme Both Express a Core Transcriptional Program During Chondrocyte Differentiation

## Citation (NLM)
Gomez-Picos P, Ovens K, Eames BF. Limb mesoderm and head ectomesenchyme both express a core transcriptional program during chondrocyte differentiation. Front Cell Dev Biol. 2022;10:876825. doi:10.3389/fcell.2022.876825

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.3389/fcell.2022.876825](https://doi.org/10.3389/fcell.2022.876825)

---

## Background

During vertebrate evolution, cartilage appeared at different times in different body regions. The skull emerged before the limbs, and one hypothesis explaining this phenomenon is that a core gene regulatory network (core GRN) driving chondrocyte differentiation was co-opted in cell populations of distinct embryonic origins (mesoderm and neural crest). During endochondral ossification, immature chondrocytes (IMM) and maturing chondrocytes (MAT) exist, with SOX9 and RUNX2 serving as their respective master regulators. However, most studies have focused on limb cartilage, leaving a comprehensive transcriptome-level comparison of whether the chondrocyte differentiation GRN is conserved across different body sites unexplored.

---

## Key Experiment Methods

1. **Tissue collection and laser capture microdissection (LCM)**: IMM and MAT cells were isolated by laser capture from the humerus (mesoderm-derived, limb) and ceratobranchial (neural crest-derived, head) of HH36 chick embryos.
2. **RNA-seq**: RNA was extracted from isolated chondrocytes and transcriptomic analysis was performed by Illumina sequencing. Biological replicates were included per condition.
3. **Venn diagram and gene ontology (GO) analysis**: Identification of shared and region-specific genes between limb and head chondrocytes, followed by enrichment analysis of biological processes.
4. **Gene co-expression network (GCN) analysis**: GCN construction from limb and head chondrocyte data using Cytoscape. Correlation estimates between IMM/MAT and limb/head states.
5. **Traditional GRN construction**: Integration of functional regulatory data from published literature with chick chondrocyte transcriptome data to build a core cartilage GRN.

---

## Results

- Limb and head chondrocytes share approximately 77% of their transcriptome (69% for IMM, 80% for MAT).
- SOX9, SOX5, SOX6, COL2A1, ACAN (classic IMM markers) and RUNX2, COL10A1, MMP13, IBSP, SPP1 (MAT markers) were highly expressed in both limb and head chondrocytes.
- GO analysis showed enrichment of cartilage differentiation-related biological processes in the shared gene set. Limb-specific genes were enriched in limb/forelimb morphogenesis, while head-specific genes were enriched in neural crest-dependent processes (cranial skeleton morphogenesis, cell migration, neural differentiation).
- GCN analysis revealed that IMM and MAT genes formed positively correlated clusters, with a negative correlation between the two clusters, reflecting the antagonistic relationship between SOX9 (repression) and RUNX2 (activation).
- The GRN showed that the SOX9 → SOX5/SOX6 → COL2A1/ACAN (IMM) and RUNX2 → COL10A1/MMP13/SPP1/IBSP (MAT) pathways operate identically in both limb and head.
- Limb-specific genes (HOXA, HOXD, TBX5, SHOX2, DLX5) and head-specific genes (PAX7, SIX1, ZIC1, ID1, GATA2/3) reflect embryonic origin but are not components of the core cartilage GRN.
- Novel potential cartilage genes were identified: IMM-associated POU3F3, CSF1, PTN, UNC5C; MAT-associated FAM20C, TCF7L2, IFITM5, among others.

---

## Perspective

- This study provides the first transcriptome-wide evidence that limb and head chondrocytes share the same core GRN driving chondrocyte differentiation, strongly supporting the GRN co-option hypothesis in evolution.
- Many genes currently classified as cartilage-related (e.g., HOX genes, PAX7, TBX5, SHOX2) may actually be merely region-specific morphogenetic competency factors. The definition of the core cartilage GRN should be restricted to genes universally required for cartilage formation, such as SOX9, COL2A1, and COL10A1.
- Newly identified candidate cartilage genes (POU3F3, CSF1, PTN, TNC, FAM20C, TCF7L2, etc.) are conserved across vertebrates and associated with osteoarthritis (OA) progression. Future loss-of-function experiments are needed for validation.
- A limitation of this study is its single time-point analysis (HH36), which does not account for timing differences in maturation between limb and head. Detailed time-course studies are needed.
