# Adult chondrogenesis and spontaneous cartilage repair in the skate, Leucoraja erinacea

## Citation (NLM)
Marconi A, Hancock-Ronemus A, Gillis JA. Adult chondrogenesis and spontaneous cartilage repair in the skate, Leucoraja erinacea. eLife. 2020;9:e53414. doi:10.7554/eLife.53414

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.7554/eLife.53414](https://doi.org/10.7554/eLife.53414)

---

## Background
Mammalian articular cartilage is an avascular tissue with poor capacity for spontaneous repair. In contrast, chondrichthyans (sharks, skates, rays) maintain a cartilaginous endoskeleton throughout life and exhibit indeterminate growth. This study investigates whether embryonic cartilage development in the skate (Leucoraja erinacea) mirrors that of mammals, and whether persistence of adult chondrogenesis correlates with the ability to spontaneously repair cartilage injuries.

## Key Experiment Methods
1. Histological analysis of skate metapterygium from embryonic stages (S30) through hatching (modified Masson's trichrome staining)
2. Chromogenic and multiplexed fluorescent mRNA in situ hybridization (HCR) for Col2a1, Agc, Sox5, Sox6, Sox9
3. EdU pulse-chase label retention experiments for cell proliferation and fate tracing (hatchlings: 1, 5, 10, 40 days; adults: 3 days, 1, 2, 5.5 months)
4. Vibrotome sectioning and histochemical analysis of adult metapterygium
5. Surgical cartilage injury model using 4mm biopsy punch with 12-month recovery monitoring
6. Immunofluorescence for Type I/II collagen to assess ECM composition of repair tissue

## Results
- Embryonic skate cartilage shares conserved features with mammals: mesenchymal condensation, chondrocyte differentiation, co-expression of Sox5/Sox6/Sox9 with Col2a1/Agc
- In hatchling metapterygium, EdU+ cells concentrated at cartilage periphery and perichondrium; EdU+ perichondral cells increased at 5-10 day chase and decreased by 40 days (suggesting self-renewing progenitor population)
- Sox5/Sox6/Sox9+ but Col2a1- flattened cells identified in inner perichondrium, representing cartilage progenitor cells
- Adult metapterygium features: hyaline cartilage core, surface tesserae (calcified cartilage plates), fibrous perichondrium, and cartilage canals extending from perichondrium into core
- Adult EdU tracing: EdU+ cells found exclusively in perichondrium at 3 days; abundant in perichondrium, cartilage canals, and peripheral chondrocytes at 5.5 months, confirming perichondral progenitors give rise to new chondrocytes
- Cartilage injury experiment: 1-2 months post-operation, injury filled with fibrous connective tissue; by 3 months, differentiation into cartilage began; by 11-12 months, injury sites completely filled with hyaline-like repair cartilage (rich in Type II collagen, no Type I collagen)
- Mechanical perturbation of perichondrium alone induced ectopic cartilage formation

## Perspective
The skate provides a unique animal model for studying adult chondrogenesis and spontaneous cartilage repair. The presence of Sox5/6/9+ progenitor cells in the perichondrium and interstitial growth via cartilage canals demonstrate mechanisms of adult cartilage growth and repair. While mammalian articular cartilage lacks such progenitor pools and cannot heal spontaneously, understanding the mechanisms by which skate progenitor cells generate cartilage could inspire novel cell-based therapies for skeletal pathologies such as osteoarthritis.
