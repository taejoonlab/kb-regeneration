# Feasibility of Human Platelet Lysate as an Alternative to Foetal Bovine Serum for In Vitro Expansion of Chondrocytes

## Citation (NLM)
Liau LL, Hassan MNFB, Tang YL, Ng MH, Law JX. Feasibility of Human Platelet Lysate as an Alternative to Foetal Bovine Serum for In Vitro Expansion of Chondrocytes. Int J Mol Sci. 2021;22(3):1269. doi:10.3390/ijms22031269

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.3390/ijms22031269](https://doi.org/10.3390/ijms22031269)

---

## Background
Osteoarthritis (OA) is a degenerative joint disease affecting many people worldwide. Autologous chondrocyte implantation (ACI) is a promising treatment modality for regenerating damaged cartilage. Traditionally, foetal bovine serum (FBS) has been used for chondrocyte expansion, but it carries risks of animal pathogen transmission and animal protein transfer, making it unsuitable for clinical applications. Human platelet lysate (HPL), rich in growth factors that enhance cell proliferation, has emerged as a potential alternative to FBS.

## Key Experiment Methods
1. Literature review comparing proliferation-promoting capacity of FBS vs. HPL for chondrocytes
2. Analysis of HPL preparation methods (fresh/expired platelet concentrates, freeze-thaw cycles, centrifugation)
3. Evaluation of strategies to prevent chondrocyte dedifferentiation (high seeding density, hypoxic environment, specific substrates, chemical compounds, MSC co-culture)
4. Comparison of redifferentiation capacity of dedifferentiated chondrocytes (3D culture, mechanical/electrical stimulation, chemical inducers)
5. Analysis of alternative culture conditions including serum-free medium (SFM), human serum (HS), and platelet-rich plasma (PRP)

## Results
- HPL consistently promotes chondrocyte proliferation more effectively than FBS (rich in PDGF, bFGF, TGF-β1, IGF-1, VEGF, HGF)
- Both HPL and FBS fail to prevent chondrocyte dedifferentiation (morphological change from rounded/cobblestone to fibroblastic, COL2A1 to COL1/COL3 switch)
- Discrepant results on redifferentiation potential: some studies show HPL-cultured chondrocytes maintain redifferentiation capacity after 10 population doublings (vs. 3 for FBS), while others report poorer redifferentiation with HPL
- These discrepancies are likely due to variations in HPL preparation methods
- Serum-free medium (SFM) eliminates batch-to-batch variation, better maintains SOX9 expression, and enables spontaneous redifferentiation in 3D culture
- Human serum (HS) is superior for proliferation but inferior to FBS in preventing dedifferentiation

## Perspective
HPL is superior to FBS in promoting chondrocyte proliferation, but its ability to maintain chondrogenic potential (reducing dedifferentiation and enhancing redifferentiation) shows inconsistent results due to differences in HPL preparation methods. Development of a standardized HPL preparation technique that reproducibly maintains chondrocyte chondrogenic potential is warranted. International standardization of HPL safety and quality criteria (infectious disease screening, pathogen inactivation) is also critical. While HPL can reduce ACI production costs, the therapeutic potential of HPL-cultured chondrocytes must be confirmed in preclinical and clinical studies before routine clinical use.
