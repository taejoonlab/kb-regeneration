# Heterogeneous fibroblasts contribute to fibrotic scar formation after spinal cord injury in mice and monkeys

## Citation (NLM)
Xue X, Wu X, Fan Y, Han S, Zhang H, Sun Y, Yin Y, Yin M, Chen B, Sun Z, Zhao S, Zhang Q, Liu W, Zhang J, Li J, Shi Y, Xiao Z, Dai J, Zhao Y. Heterogeneous fibroblasts contribute to fibrotic scar formation after spinal cord injury in mice and monkeys. Nat Commun. 2024;15:6321. doi:10.1038/s41467-024-50564-x

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-024-50564-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-024-50564-x)

---

## Background

Spinal cord injury (SCI) generates two types of scars at the lesion site: astrocytic scars (proliferating GFAP+ astrocytes) and fibrotic scars filled with ECM-producing myofibroblasts. Fibrotic scars are considered barriers to axon regeneration, yet complete ablation produces a detrimental cavity while partial reduction enhances recovery—implying functional heterogeneity of fibrotic scar tissue. The cellular origin of scar-forming myofibroblasts has been controversial, variously attributed to meningeal fibroblasts (MFs), perivascular fibroblasts (PFs), or type A pericytes, partly due to a lack of distinguishing marker genes.

This study dissects the distribution, origin, and function of fibroblasts within fibrotic scars after SCI in mice and female rhesus monkeys, using lineage tracing and single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq).

---

## Key Experiment Methods

1. Lineage tracing with multiple CreER::R26-TdTomato mouse lines: PDGFRβ-CreER (perivascular + meningeal cells), Col1a2-CreER (fibroblasts), Myh11-CreER and NG2-CreER (pericytes/vSMCs), and a CRISPR/Cas9-generated Crabp2-CreER (meningeal fibroblasts).
2. Two SCI models: transection (penetrating) and crush (non-penetrating), analyzed at 5 dpi, 14 dpi, and 1 month post-injury.
3. Immunostaining for TdT co-localization with CD31/Pdx (vessels), GFAP, CD68, Ki67/BrdU (proliferation), Acta2/Tagln (myofibroblast), Desmin, Lama1/Lama2, Crabp2/Emb, Col1/Col6a1.
4. scRNA-seq of FACS-sorted PDGFRβ-TdT+ cells (44,386 cells from 0, 5, 14 dpi); clustering, DEG analysis, and comparison with published crush-SCI datasets.
5. Signaling pathway analysis: GO/KEGG enrichment and CellPhoneDB ligand-receptor interaction scoring (TGF-β, Wnt, PDGF).
6. Monkey (Macaca mulatta) transection SCI with 10x Genomics sequencing of lesion edge and core; cross-species DEG comparison and immunostaining (CRABP2, LAMA1).
7. In vitro assays: isolated CE-F (dura/arachnoid) and LA-F (pia) tested for cholesterol synthesis/transport genes, Bodipy lipid droplets, myelin phagocytosis, and transwell co-culture angiogenesis.

---

## Results

- PDGFRβ+ cells (perivascular and meningeal) migrate into the lesion core and convert into Acta2+ myofibroblasts after both transection and crush SCI; recruitment is higher in transection than crush injury.
- Fibroblasts, not pericytes/vSMCs, are the primary source of fibrotic scars: <8% of TdT+ cells co-localized with Desmin; Myh11+ and NG2+ pericytes/vSMCs contributed minimally, whereas Col1a2-CreER-traced fibroblasts filled the Col1+/Col6a1+ lesion.
- Two fibroblast populations were resolved: meningeal Crabp2+/Emb+ fibroblasts (CE-F) localized to the central region of the scar, and perivascular/pial Lama1+/Lama2+ fibroblasts (LA-F) at the periphery.
- CE-F show enhanced cholesterol synthesis (Hmgcr, Pmvk, Hmgcs1) and secrete type I collagen and fibronectin; LA-F express laminin and type IV collagen and function in cholesterol transport (Apod, Apoe, Abca1, Lrp1) and angiogenesis.
- Signaling: TGF-β and Wnt pathways are primarily activated in fibroblasts and drive fibrosis, while PDGFB/PDGFD signaling is more active in pericytes/vSMCs (angiogenesis). Microglia/macrophages secrete TGFB ligands that activate TGF-β signaling in fibroblasts.
- LA-F preferentially phagocytosed myelin and formed lipid droplets in vitro and in vivo, and promoted vessel formation in co-culture more than CE-F.
- The fibroblast heterogeneity (central CE-F, peripheral LA-F) is conserved between mice and monkeys; more CE-F were recruited to the larger monkey lesion core.

---

## Perspective

By combining rigorous multi-line lineage tracing with cross-species scRNA-seq, the study resolves a long-standing controversy, showing that spinal cord fibrotic scars derive predominantly from PDGFRβ+ meningeal and perivascular fibroblasts—not pericytes/vSMCs—in both penetrating and non-penetrating injury. It defines two spatially and functionally distinct scar fibroblast subtypes (CE-F for cholesterol synthesis/collagen deposition; LA-F for lipid transport/angiogenesis), providing a framework for selectively remodeling rather than wholly ablating the fibrotic scar to improve axon regeneration and functional recovery.

Limitations include the acknowledgment that Myh11+/NG2+ lines do not label all pericytes (so a minor pericyte-to-myofibroblast contribution cannot be fully excluded) and pericyte heterogeneity in the CNS remains incompletely characterized. Future work should test subtype-specific ablation or modulation of CE-F versus LA-F to determine their differential effects on the injured microenvironment and recovery.
