# Leg regeneration is epigenetically regulated by histone H3K27 methylation in the cricket Gryllus bimaculatus

## Citation (NLM)
Hamada Y, Bando T, Nakamura T, Ishimaru Y, Mito T, Noji S, Tomioka K, Ohuchi H. Leg regeneration is epigenetically regulated by histone H3K27 methylation in the cricket Gryllus bimaculatus. Development. 2015;142(16):2916-2927. doi:10.1242/dev.122598

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1242/dev.122598](https://doi.org/10.1242/dev.122598)

---

## Background

Hemimetabolous insects such as the two-spotted cricket *Gryllus bimaculatus* regenerate lost distal leg parts using blastemal cells — a population of dedifferentiated, proliferating cells that later redifferentiate to restore tissue through a repatterning process guided by positional information and patterning genes. Blastema and differentiated cells display distinct gene expression profiles, implicating epigenetic factors in controlling the transitions between these cell states during regeneration.

Methylation of histone H3 lysine 27 (H3K27) is a repressive mark deposited by Enhancer of zeste [E(z), a PRC2 methyltransferase] and removed by Utx (a JmjC-domain demethylase). A prior comparative transcriptome analysis by the authors found several epigenetic modifiers upregulated in the cricket blastema (Utx showed the highest blastema/non-regenerative RPKM ratio of 8.9). This study tests the functional roles of the *Gryllus* homologues Gb'E(z) and Gb'Utx in H3K27 methylation dynamics and leg repatterning during regeneration.

---

## Key Experiment Methods

1. **Gene identification and phylogenetics** — Cloned full-length Gb'E(z) (746 aa, C-terminal SET domain) and Gb'Utx (1443 aa, C-terminal JmjC domain) from transcriptome data; sequence alignment and phylogenetic tree construction across Drosophila, mouse, human.
2. **Whole-mount in situ hybridization** — Expression patterns of Gb'E(z), Gb'Utx and leg patterning genes (Gb'dac, Gb'Egfr, Gb'BarH, Gb'Dll) in regenerating legs (6 days post-amputation, dpa).
3. **RNAi knockdown** — dsRNA against Gb'E(z) and Gb'Utx (plus non-overlapping "_C" region dsRNAs to control for off-target effects); knockdown confirmed by qPCR.
4. **Immunostaining** — H3K27me3 detection in regenerating legs of control, Gb'E(z)RNAi, and Gb'UtxRNAi crickets at 2 and 6 dpa.
5. **Morphological/amputation analyses** — Regenerated leg phenotype scoring (classes 1–3) at metathoracic (T3) and mesothoracic (T2) legs; amputation at distal/middle/proximal tibia positions; grafting experiments to induce supernumerary legs.
6. **Cross-species and timing tests** — RNAi against E(z) homologue in a second cricket species (Modicogryllus siamensis); RNAi time-window experiments (0–48 h post-amputation) and RNAi applied 72 h before amputation to test dedifferentiation involvement; DNA methylation machinery RNAi (Gb'Dnmt2, Gb'Dnmt3, Gb'Tet).

---

## Results

- **Both genes are expressed in the blastema** and regulate H3K27me3: Gb'E(z)RNAi decreased H3K27me3-positive nuclei, whereas Gb'UtxRNAi increased them, confirming their opposing methyltransferase/demethylase roles.
- **Gb'E(z) suppresses extra tibia formation:** Gb'E(z)RNAi regenerated legs formed an extra tibia-like segment between the tibia and tarsus (class 3, most severe), with reconstructed tibial spurs. The phenotype rate increased with more proximal amputation (up to 62% class 3 at proximal vs. 14% at distal tibia).
- **Mechanism via Gb'dac:** The Gb'dac expression domain expanded in the proximal tarsus of Gb'E(z)RNAi legs, and this ectopic derepression correlated with extra tibia segment formation; Gb'BarH and Gb'Dll expression were unaltered.
- **Gb'Utx promotes tarsus joint formation:** Gb'UtxRNAi legs showed tarsal spur and Ta1–Ta2 leg-joint defects, associated with diminished Gb'Egfr expression in the middle of the tarsus.
- **Conserved across cricket species:** Ms'E(z)RNAi in M. siamensis reproduced the extra tibia segment phenotype.
- **Not required for dedifferentiation:** RNAi applied 72 h before amputation caused no regeneration defects; both effects acted within ~8 h post-amputation, indicating H3K27me3 control operates during repatterning, not blastema formation — differing from the roles reported in mouse, zebrafish, and Drosophila.
- **DNA methylation is dispensable:** RNAi against Gb'Dnmt2/3 or Gb'Tet produced no phenotype, indicating H3K27 methylation, not DNA methylation, is the dominant epigenetic repression mode in cricket leg regeneration.

---

## Perspective

The study establishes that precise control of leg patterning gene expression via histone H3K27me3 is essential for correct repatterning during cricket leg regeneration: Gb'E(z) restrains segment identity (preventing extra tibia formation via Gb'dac repression) while Gb'Utx enables joint restoration (via Gb'Egfr derepression). This provides a mechanistic link between chromatin state and positional patterning in an invertebrate regeneration model with dedifferentiation-based blastema formation.

A notable divergence from vertebrate and Drosophila systems is that H3K27me3 dynamics are not required for dedifferentiation/blastema formation in the cricket but instead fine-tune redifferentiation and repatterning; likewise, DNA methylation appears dispensable. Limitations include the RNAi-based (rather than genetic) loss of function and the incomplete penetrance of phenotypes, likely reflecting redundant molecular systems. Future work could address contributions of other histone marks (H3K4, H3K9, H3K36) and acetylation, the role of H3K9 methylation, and the broader evolutionary logic of epigenetic control across regenerating taxa.
