# Reactive oligodendrocyte progenitor cells (re-)myelinate the regenerating zebrafish spinal cord

## Citation (NLM)
Tsata V, Kroehne V, Wehner D, Rost F, Lange C, Hoppe C, Kurth T, Reinhardt S, Petzold A, Dahl A, Loeffler M, Reimer MM, Brand M. Reactive oligodendrocyte progenitor cells (re-)myelinate the regenerating zebrafish spinal cord. Development. 2020;147(24):dev193946. doi:10.1242/dev.193946

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1242/dev.193946](https://doi.org/10.1242/dev.193946)

---

## Background

Spinal cord injury (SCI) results in loss of neurons, oligodendrocytes and myelin sheaths, which in mammals are not efficiently restored. Death of mature myelinating oligodendrocytes is one of the first events after SCI, and the resulting scarcity of oligodendrocytes at the lesion impairs re-myelination of spared fibres, leaving axons denuded, impeding signal conduction and contributing to permanent functional deficits. In mammals, limited re-myelination can occur via recruitment of oligodendrocyte progenitor cells (OPCs), but oligodendrocyte replacement is generally incomplete owing to progenitor depletion, age-related impairment of OPC recruitment/differentiation, axonal dystrophy and the inhibitory environment of the injury scar.

In contrast to mammals, adult zebrafish efficiently regenerate the spinal cord and regain swimming function by ~42 days post lesion (dpl). Although oligodendroglial lineage progression and myelination are largely conserved between zebrafish and mammals, the behaviour of OPCs/oligodendrocytes after SCI in a regeneration-permissive vertebrate had not been analysed in detail. This study characterizes oligodendroglial lineage cell dynamics and re-myelination in an adult zebrafish spinal cord transection model.

---

## Key Experiment Methods

1. Adult zebrafish spinal cord transection model with sham controls; analysis at 3, 7, 14, 42 dpl and up to 1 year post lesion (ypl).
2. Transgenic reporter lines: Tg(mbp:eGFP) to label mature myelinating oligodendrocytes and myelin sheaths, and Tg(olig2:eGFP) to label OPCs and a subset of ventricular radial glia.
3. Stereological quantification of oligodendrocytes/OPCs along the rostrocaudal axis around the transection site.
4. TUNEL assay to quantify dying cells (from 4 hpl through 7 dpl); L-plastin staining for immune cells.
5. Transmission electron microscopy (TEM) for ultrastructural axon counts, percentage of myelinated axons and G-ratio analysis; Toluidine Blue semi-thin sections; X-ray microtomography (XμCT) at 1 ypl.
6. Proliferation assays: anti-PCNA immunostaining and EdU labelling with CUBIC-based whole-mount tissue clearing.
7. Immunostaining for Claudin K (mature oligodendrocytes/myelin) and Neurofilament (axons).
8. Transcriptional profiling: FAC-sorting of olig2:eGFP+ OPCs from ~0.5 mm lesion-site tissue at 7 dpl, RNA-seq of reactive vs non-reactive OPCs, GO analysis; validation by fluorescent in situ hybridization (apoeb, ctgfa, sema3c, tnc).
9. Single-cell RNA-seq of reactive OPCs.

---

## Results

- mbp:eGFP+ oligodendrocytes were significantly reduced at 3 and 7 dpl within a spatially confined ~700 µm lesion zone (±350 µm), with damaged myelin sheaths; numbers returned to pre-injury levels by 14 and 42 dpl.
- TUNEL showed very few mbp:eGFP+/TUNEL+ or olig2:eGFP+/TUNEL+ cells (<2% of the OPC population), suggesting oligodendrocytes and OPCs largely survive the injury rather than dying.
- TEM: total axon number was reduced ~50% at 7 dpl and remained lower at 42 dpl; percentage of myelinated axons dropped and only partially recovered by 42 dpl. Newly formed myelin was thinner (altered G-ratio), and (re-)myelination varied between individual fish. Macrophages/microglia engulfed myelin/lipid debris.
- Tissue architecture remained altered: enlarged central canal and increased vascularization persisted at 42 dpl and even at 1 ypl; severed Mauthner axons did not recover. Thus functional recovery at 42 dpl coincides with only partially restored axonal and myelination profiles.
- OPCs entered a reactive state rapidly (92.5% reactive at 3 dpl), with enlarged cell bodies and long multi-branched processes; morphology returned to baseline by 42 dpl.
- OPCs increased proliferation (PCNA+, EdU+) within the lesion zone; ~10% of OPCs retained EdU at 14 dpl. Both reactive and non-reactive OPCs proliferate.
- SCI induced de novo generation of mbp:eGFP+/EdU+ oligodendrocytes by 14 dpl; some reactive OPCs expressed Claudin K while apposed to axons, indicating differentiation into (pre-)myelinating oligodendrocytes.
- RNA-seq of reactive OPCs at 7 dpl identified 1755 DEGs (1124 up, 631 down). Reactive OPCs upregulated genes for migration, cell adhesion, cytoskeletal remodelling, cell cycle/differentiation, lipid metabolism, transport, compact-myelin formation, and several immune-related genes (e.g., tnfrsf11a, cxcl14, il4r.1, stat6). scRNA-seq showed distinct reactive-OPC clusters (functional heterogeneity). FISH validated apoeb, ctgfa, sema3c and tnc upregulation.

## Perspective

The study provides the first detailed characterization of oligodendroglial biology and the first reactive-OPC transcriptome in a regeneration-competent vertebrate spinal cord. It shows that adult zebrafish OPCs are a robust population that survives SCI, becomes reactive, proliferates and re-establishes the oligodendrocyte pool within 2 weeks, restoring myelination sufficiently for functional recovery even though axon number and myelination remain incomplete. Notably, oligodendrocyte re-establishment appears to occur not only through proliferation but also via direct differentiation of reactive OPCs, and inflammation appears coupled to OPC activation—consistent with reports that inflammation is required for regeneration in zebrafish CNS.

Limitations acknowledged by the authors include the lack of Cre-based lineage tracing (precluding definitive discrimination between de novo myelination of regrown axons, re-myelination of spared axons, and possible de-differentiation of spared oligodendrocytes), possible loss of GFP-reporter signal masking dying cells, and small numbers of EdU-retaining oligodendrocytes. Future work should determine myelin sheath number/length per oligodendrocyte and the extent of complete (re-)myelination over longer recovery windows. The findings suggest functional repair may require only a threshold of axonal myelin support rather than complete re-myelination, and highlight zebrafish OPC responses as a model for enhancing myelin repair after CNS injury in mammals.
