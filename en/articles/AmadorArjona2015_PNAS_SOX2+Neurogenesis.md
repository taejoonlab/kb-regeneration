# SOX2 primes the epigenetic landscape in neural precursors enabling proper gene activation during hippocampal neurogenesis

## Citation (NLM)

Amador-Arjona A, Cimadamore F, Huang CT, Wright R, Lewis S, Gage FH, Terskikh AV. SOX2 primes the epigenetic landscape in neural precursors enabling proper gene activation during hippocampal neurogenesis. Proc Natl Acad Sci U S A. 2015;112(15):E1936-E1945. doi:10.1073/pnas.1421480112

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1421480112](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1421480112)

---

## Background

Newborn granule neurons generated from neural progenitor cells (NPCs) in the adult hippocampal subgranular zone contribute to spatial learning and pattern separation, but the molecular mechanisms controlling activation of their neurogenic program are poorly understood. SOX2, a SOXB1 transcription factor and hallmark of neural stem/progenitor cells, is required for NPC self-renewal, and its mutation in humans causes anophthalmia, defective hippocampal development, seizures, and intellectual disability. However, SOX2's role in neuronal differentiation had been paradoxical: SOX2 overexpression represses differentiation and it was viewed largely as a repressor of neuronal genes, yet loss-of-function studies show SOX2 is required for neurogenesis.

In NPCs, proneural/neurogenic genes carry bivalent chromatin marks (both repressive H3K27me3 and activating H3K4me3) that keep them silent but poised for activation once differentiation cues arrive. This study tested a novel hypothesis: that SOX2 binds these poised, bivalently marked promoters and maintains a permissive epigenetic state—by limiting polycomb repressive complex 2 (PRC2) activity—thereby enabling proper gene activation at the onset of hippocampal neurogenesis.

---

## Key Experiment Methods

1. Genome-wide analysis of published ChIP-seq data (SOX2 binding, H3K27me3, H3K4me3) in mouse ES cell-derived NPCs to identify bivalently marked SOX2-binding sites; Gene Ontology classification.
2. In vitro SOX2 loss-of-function in adult hippocampal NPCs (hipNPCs) using doxycycline-inducible shRNA against Sox2 (shSox2) vs. control (shCTRL).
3. ChIP-qPCR for SOX2, H3K27me3, H3K4me3, H3K9ac, EZH2, and GCN5 at proneural/neurogenic loci (Ngn2, NeuroD1, Sox21, Bdnf, Gadd45b); Western blots for global H3K27me3/EZH2.
4. Neurogenic induction with Wnt3a and qRT-PCR of target genes; rescue attempts with HDAC inhibitor valproic acid (VPA).
5. Genetic mouse model: Sox2flox::mGFAP-Cre::nestin-CFPnuc (SOX2cKO) with postnatal deletion in adult NPCs; immunohistochemistry for NGN2, NEUROD1, PROX1, DCX, GFAP, CFPnuc.
6. In vivo neurogenesis quantification: PCNA, BrdU (2 h and 4-week protocols), CldU/IdU cell-cycle reentry, NeuN co-labeling; apoptosis by activated caspase-3 and TUNEL.
7. Rescue of the phenotype by lentiviral/retroviral NeuroD1 delivery in vitro and in vivo (dual-DG retroviral injection with mCherry internal control).
8. Morphological and electrophysiological analysis of newborn neurons (dendritic length/branching, spine density/morphology, whole-cell recordings of sEPSCs).

---

## Results

- Of 16,683 SOX2-binding sites in NPCs, 2,514 (15.1%) were enriched for H3K27me3, of which 680 (27%) were bivalent (also H3K4me3); these constituted 25% of all bivalently marked promoters. SOX2-bound bivalent genes encode early neurogenic regulators, whereas the 75% of bivalent promoters lacking SOX2 are involved in later neuronal maturation. H3K27me3 density showed a "valley" flanking SOX2-binding sites.
- SOX2 bound the regulatory regions of poised proneural (Ngn2, NeuroD1) and neurogenic (Sox21, Bdnf, Gadd45b) genes. Sox2 knockdown selectively increased H3K27me3 and EZH2 binding at these SOX2 sites (without changing global H3K27me3/EZH2), while H3K4me3 was unaffected—shifting the chromatin toward a repressed state.
- Wnt3a-induced activation of Ngn2 and NeuroD1 (and Sox21, Bdnf, Gadd45b) was markedly impaired in SOX2-deficient hipNPCs. HDAC inhibitor VPA failed to rescue expression, indicating a profound, acetylation-insensitive chromatin change; GCN5-dependent H3K9ac deposition was also reduced.
- In vivo, SOX2cKO mice showed reduced NGN2 (amplifying progenitors) and NEUROD1 (early neuroblasts) expression, with loss of the normal early-neuroblast NEUROD1 peak. PROX1 (bound by SOX2 but not bivalently marked) was unaffected, confirming specificity for poised genes.
- SOX2cKO caused an age-progressive reduction in NPCs, amplifying progenitors, neuroblasts (DCX+), and mature newborn neurons (~45.5% fewer BrdU+NeuN+ cells); radial NPC self-renewal was only slightly affected. The dominant defect was increased apoptosis of neuroblasts (elevated AC3+/TUNEL+ DCX+ cells).
- Retroviral NeuroD1 delivery rescued neuroblast survival/neurogenesis in SOX2cKO mice in vitro and in vivo, demonstrating that restoring a downstream proneural gene is sufficient.
- Surviving SOX2-deficient newborn neurons showed aberrant maturation: reduced dendritic length and branching, decreased overall spine density but increased mushroom-spine density, and increased sEPSC amplitude—revealing a "delayed" role for SOX2 in neuronal maturation long after its expression ceases.

---

## Perspective

This study reveals a previously unrecognized epigenetic function of SOX2: acting as a lineage-specific transcription factor that maintains the bivalent/poised chromatin state at early neurogenic genes by locally limiting PRC2 (EZH2/H3K27me3) activity, thereby licensing robust gene activation upon neurogenic cues. It reconciles the paradox of SOX2 as both a repressor of premature differentiation and a factor required for neurogenesis—SOX2 does not activate these genes directly but keeps them poised. To the authors' knowledge this is the first example of a lineage-specific factor maintaining bivalency at poised promoters to coordinate the onset of a developmental program.

The findings have direct relevance to human SOX2 anophthalmia syndrome, in which hippocampal anomalies and learning/memory deficits are common, and to broader efforts in neural regeneration and reprogramming (SOX2 can reprogram fibroblasts to NSCs and, with Mash1, pericytes to neurons). The demonstration that a single downstream proneural gene (NeuroD1) can rescue survival points toward possible interventions. Limitations the authors note include that the genome-wide bivalency/SOX2 maps were derived from ES cell-derived NPCs rather than hipNPCs, and that the precise mechanism of SOX2-PRC2 interference (steric competition, cofactor recruitment, DNA methylation, or chromatin remodeling) remains to be defined. Future work should generate hipNPC-specific epigenomic maps and identify the downstream effectors of this poised-state maintenance.
