# Ttc30a affects tubulin modifications in a model for ciliary chondrodysplasia with polycystic kidney disease

## Citation (NLM)
Getwan M, Hoppmann A, Schlosser P, Grand K, Song W, Diehl R, Schroda S, Heeg F, Deutsch K, Hildebrandt F, Lausch E, Köttgen A, Lienkamp SS. Ttc30a affects tubulin modifications in a model for ciliary chondrodysplasia with polycystic kidney disease. Proc Natl Acad Sci USA. 2021;118(37):e2106770118. doi:10.1073/pnas.2106770118

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2106770118](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2106770118)

## Background

Skeletal ciliopathies (SCs), such as Jeune syndrome, short rib polydactyly syndrome, and Sensenbrenner syndrome, are characterized by skeletal abnormalities and frequently accompanied by nephronophthisis-like cystic kidney disease. Although more than 27 disease genes have been identified, the common molecular mechanisms linking cartilage defects and cystic kidney disease remain unclear. Dysfunction of the primary cilium disrupts Hedgehog (Hh) signaling, thereby affecting chondrocyte proliferation and differentiation. Post-translational modifications (PTMs) of axonemal tubulin—particularly polyglycylation and polyglutamylation—are critical for ciliary function, yet how defects in these PTMs contribute to phenotypic heterogeneity in ciliopathies is unknown.

---

## Key Experiment Methods

1. **CRISPR/Cas9 gene editing**: Cas9/sgRNA ribonucleoprotein (RNP) injection into 1-cell or 2-cell stage X. tropicalis embryos. Target genes included ift80, ift172, ttc30a, and candidate genes (cfap20, c11orf74, cep192).
2. **Phenotypic analysis**: Quantification of edema, limb length measurement, and microCT contrast-enhanced scanning for 3D analysis of skeletal and kidney structures.
3. **Histology and immunostaining**: H&E staining and immunofluorescence using antibodies against acetylated tubulin, polyglutamylation, and monoglycylation.
4. **Kidney excretory function analysis**: Real-time observation of fluorescently labeled dextran movement through renal tubules after intracoelomic injection.
5. **In silico screening by data mining**: Identification of candidate genes sharing common molecular features with known SC genes from 90 public genome/proteome resource lists.
6. **RNA-seq**: Transcriptomic analysis of ift80- and ift172-targeted embryos (neurula stage 22). Confirmed changes in Hh and Wnt signaling pathway genes.
7. **Single-cell RNA-seq analysis**: Analysis of publicly available data from E13.5 mouse limb buds. Expression patterns of Ttc30a1, Ttc30a2, and Ttc30b.

## Results

- CRISPR/Cas9 targeting of ift80 and ift172 in X. tropicalis froglets produced severe limb deformities (shortening, syndactyly, polydactyly) and cystic kidneys (mesonephric polycystic kidney disease).
- Limb cartilage showed a predominance of resting and proliferative zones with reduced hypertrophic and ossification zones, indicating defective chondrocyte differentiation.
- ift80 and ift172 deficiency reduced the length and number of cilia in multiciliated cells (MCCs) and markedly impaired renal excretory function (dextran excretion rate 12–14% vs. 85% in controls).
- RNA-seq detected changes in Hh signaling (ccng1, ulk1, riok3) and Wnt signaling-related genes.
- In silico screening identified 65 candidate genes. Among them, ttc30a targeting recapitulated all ift80 and ift172 loss-of-function phenotypes (edema, limb shortening, polydactyly, cystic kidneys, reduced ciliary length).
- Ttc30a-deficient cilia showed reduced tubulin glycylation, loss of acetylation, and defective axoneme compartmentalization. Loss of acetylated tubulin at the ciliary tip was particularly prominent.
- Single-cell RNA-seq of mouse limbs showed high expression of Ttc30b and Ttc30a1 in chondrocyte and osteoblast clusters, with distribution patterns similar to Ift80 and Ift172.
- TTC30A/B acts as a key node for ciliary segmentation, suggesting that tubulin PTMs and ciliary compartmentalization contribute to cell type-specific ciliopathy manifestations.

---

## Perspective

- This study establishes X. tropicalis as a suitable model for skeletal ciliopathy research; CRISPR/Cas9 targeting faithfully recapitulates clinical phenotypes, complementing existing zebrafish and mouse models.
- Identifies TTC30A/B as a novel core component in the network of ciliary chondrodysplasia and nephronophthisis-like disease.
- Suggests that defects in tubulin PTMs (particularly glycylation and acetylation) and ciliary tip compartmentalization contribute to the phenotypic pleiotropy of skeletal and renal ciliopathies.
- Human TTC30A/B are conserved core loci that arose through gene duplication in the tetrapod lineage and are highly conserved from Chlamydomonas to humans.
- Future studies should investigate the mechanisms by which TTC30A/B regulate Hh and Wnt signaling, and their role in cystic kidney formation. Assigning in vivo tissue-specific functions to in vitro-characterized ciliary proteins remains a key challenge.
