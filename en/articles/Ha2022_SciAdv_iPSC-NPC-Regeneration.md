# Intermediate cells of in vitro cellular reprogramming and in vivo tissue regeneration require desmoplakin

## Citation (NLM)
Ha J, Kim BS, Min B, Nam J, Lee JG, Lee M, Yoon BH, Choi YH, Im I, Park JS, Choi H, Baek A, Cho SM, Lee MO, Nam KH, Mun JY, Kim M, Kim SY, Son MY, Kang YK, Lee JS, Kim JK, Kim J. Intermediate cells of in vitro cellular reprogramming and in vivo tissue regeneration require desmoplakin. Sci Adv. 2022;8(43):eabk1239. doi:10.1126/sciadv.abk1239

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1126/sciadv.abk1239](https://doi.org/10.1126/sciadv.abk1239)

---

## Background

Amphibians and fish regenerate lost body parts through dedifferentiation of somatic cells into a blastema, a mass of multipotent cells that redifferentiate into the lineages of the damaged tissue. In vitro cellular reprogramming (Yamanaka factors OSKM) has been proposed to share common processes with in vivo regeneration, since reprogramming/pluripotency factors are transiently up-regulated in the developing blastema and are required for regeneration. However, the intermediate cells (ICs) that appear transiently during reprogramming, and the molecular link between reprogramming and regeneration, remained poorly characterized.

This study set out to characterize the ICs of OSKM-mediated reprogramming and to identify a shared factor linking mammalian reprogramming with lower-vertebrate tissue regeneration.

---

## Key Experiment Methods

1. Comparative transcriptomic analysis (microarray, PCA/GSEA/ClueGO GO-term networks) of four OSKM reprogramming regimes from secondary (2°) and 4F2A mouse embryonic fibroblasts: iPSC reprogramming (iPSCR) and pluripotency factor–mediated direct reprogramming (PDR) to induced neural stem/progenitor cells (iNPCs) and dopaminergic progenitors (iDPs), sampled every 2 days.
2. Transmission electron microscopy (TEM) to detect mature desmosomes at the intermediate phase (day 6).
3. Desmoplakin (Dsp) loss/gain of function: IPTG-inducible shRNA knockdown (shDsp) during iPSCR/iNPCR and DSP overexpression in human fibroblasts; alkaline phosphatase, Nanog and Pax6 colony quantification.
4. Single-cell RNA-seq (scRNA-seq) of 33,966 cells across reprogramming time points; trajectory inference with Slingshot and Waddington optimal transport (WOT).
5. In vivo zebrafish fin regeneration assays: dspa/dspb morpholino knockdown and CRISPR sgRNA mosaic knockout in larval fin fold and adult fin, with EdU/pH3 proliferation and whole-mount in situ hybridization for blastema markers.
6. Cross-species comparison of desmosome and pluripotency signatures with published zebrafish fin and axolotl limb regeneration scRNA-seq data.

---

## Results

- iPSCR and PDR share a common transcriptomic pathway until day 6, then bifurcate; day 6 was defined as the "intermediate phase."
- Intermediate phase–specific genes (group IV, 244 common genes) were strongly enriched for epidermis/desmosome signatures; desmosomal genes (Dsp, Pkp1) and cytokeratins peaked at day 6 but fell in iPSCs, differing from canonical MET epithelial genes.
- Mature desmosomes formed transiently only at day 6 (TEM), a general feature across reprogramming systems (2° MEF, 4F2A MEF, 2° B cells, human epithelial cells).
- Dsp knockdown impaired iPSC and iNPC generation and reduced IC aggregate formation; DSP overexpression enhanced reprogramming efficiency. Dsp acts via Akt signaling (Dsp KD lowered phospho-Akt; Akt inhibitor MK2206 abrogated IC formation and reprogramming).
- Two sequential IC populations were identified: desmosomal-component–expressing ICs (dICs; Shisa8+/Dsp+) that convert into highly proliferative granular ICs (gICs; Shisa8+/Dsp−). gICs can reattach and form iPSC-like colonies but rarely express pluripotency markers—distinct from iPSCs.
- scRNA-seq found no iPSC-like intermediates during PDR, arguing that transient OSKM establishes a unique plastic state rather than passing through a pluripotent state.
- In zebrafish, dsp knockdown reduced larval fin fold regrowth (~26%), blastema cell proliferation, and blastema marker expression (junbb, msx1b, fgf20a, her6, hspd1); adult fin regeneration was reduced similarly to pou5f3/oct4 knockdown.
- gICs mapped transcriptomically to blastemal cells; sequential desmosome→pluripotency signature dynamics during reprogramming were mirrored in zebrafish and axolotl regeneration.

---

## Perspective

The work identifies desmoplakin (Dsp) as a shared, evolutionarily conserved factor bridging mammalian in vitro reprogramming and lower-vertebrate in vivo tissue regeneration, and proposes that granular ICs (gICs) may be a mammalian equivalent of blastemal cells. It offers a mechanistic clue to why mammals lost the broad regenerative capacity of fish and amphibians and suggests that specifying such intermediate cells could open new avenues for regenerative medicine. Limitations include incomplete characterization of gIC identity, reliance on morpholino/overexpression tools, and cross-species comparisons drawn from transcriptomic signature mapping rather than direct lineage tracing. Future work should define the full molecular identity and therapeutic potential of blastema-like intermediate cells.
