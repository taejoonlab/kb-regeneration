# Exaggerated inflammatory environment decreases BMP-2/ACS-induced ectopic bone mass in a rat model: implications for clinical use of BMP-2

## Citation (NLM)

Huang RL, Yuan Y, Tu J, Zou GM, Li Q. Exaggerated inflammatory environment decreases BMP-2/ACS-induced ectopic bone mass in a rat model: implications for clinical use of BMP-2. Osteoarthritis Cartilage. 2014;22(8):1186-1196. doi:10.1016/j.joca.2014.06.017

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1016/j.joca.2014.06.017](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.joca.2014.06.017)

---

## Background

Recombinant human bone morphogenetic protein-2 (rhBMP-2), delivered on an absorbable collagen sponge (ACS) as the FDA-approved INFUSE Bone Graft, is a potent osteoinductive agent used in spinal fusion, open tibial fractures, and maxillofacial applications. However, extended clinical use has revealed inflammation-related adverse events (soft-tissue swelling, seroma, bone resorption) and disappointing long-term outcomes attributed to low osteoinductive efficacy. The effects of inflammation on bone regeneration are biphasic: a proper initial inflammatory response is needed to guide regeneration, whereas excessive inflammation inhibits it. Prior in vitro work showed that LPS-induced inflammation and cytokines such as TNF-α suppress BMP-2-induced osteoblastogenesis, but the in vivo situation is more complex. This study tested the hypothesis that an exaggerated inflammatory environment decreases BMP-2/ACS-induced bone mass in vivo.

---

## Key Experiment Methods

**1. Rat ectopic bone / exaggerated inflammation model**
- 37 rats allocated to LPS-injected, saline-injected, and sham-operated groups
- LPS 5 mg/kg (or saline) injected subcutaneously daily for 4 days to mimic a pre-operative inflammatory environment
- rhBMP-2/ACS implants (4 μg rhBMP-2 per 10×10×5 mm type I bovine collagen sponge) placed in dorsal subcutaneous pockets
- Specimens harvested on days 3, 8, 28, and 56 after implantation

**2. Serum cytokine and bone-turnover profiling**
- Bio-Plex Th1/Th2 cytokine panel (IL-1β, IL-6, TNF-α, GM-CSF, IFN-γ, etc.) on serum at days 0 and 4
- ELISA for the three most elevated cytokines (TNF-α, IL-6, IL-1β) and bone-turnover markers (CTX-1, TRACP-5b, OCN) plus exogenous BMP-2

**3. Histology, histomorphometry, and micro-CT**
- H&E and Van Gieson staining for inflammation and bone structure; TRAP staining to count osteoclasts
- Immunohistochemistry for OCN
- Micro-CT of day 28/56 implants: bone volume (BV), bone mineral density (BMD), trabecular thickness/number/separation, bone mineral content (BMC = BV × BMD)

**4. In vitro osteoblast differentiation of BMSCs**
- Rat bone-marrow stromal cells treated with BMP-2 ± TNF-α, IL-6, or IL-1β
- ALP staining/activity, Alizarin red mineralization, and qRT-PCR of COL1A1, OCN, OPN, Runx2, RANKL, OPG

---

## Results

**LPS + BMP-2/ACS provoked an exaggerated inflammatory response**
Serum TNF-α, IL-6, and IL-1β were the most extensively elevated cytokines, peaking days 2–7 in LPS-injected rats and far exceeding the shorter, lower elevations seen with BMP-2/ACS alone (saline group). Exogenous BMP-2 rose ~200-fold after implantation regardless of LPS, confirming that inflammation does not impair rhBMP-2 release from ACS. Histology showed progressive inflammatory-cell infiltration expanding through the implants from days 3 to 8.

**Exaggerated inflammation decreased BMP-2/ACS-induced bone mass**
Micro-CT showed the LPS group formed significantly smaller mineral nodules with lower bone volume and mineral content at both 4 and 8 weeks than the saline group. BMD was significantly lower at 4 weeks, indicating that the inflammatory environment both suppressed formation and activated resorption.

**Bone-turnover markers shifted toward resorption**
OCN (IHC and mRNA) and Runx2 were significantly decreased in LPS-injected implants, indicating inhibited osteoblast differentiation. Simultaneously, serum CTX-1 (resorption marker) increased and RANKL mRNA rose while OPG was unchanged, raising the RANKL/OPG ratio. Notably TRAP-positive osteoclast counts and serum TRACP-5b were altered consistent with up-regulated resorption activity.

**TNF-α and IL-1β, but not IL-6, inhibited BMP-2-induced osteoblast differentiation**
In BMSC cultures, TNF-α and IL-1β dose-dependently suppressed BMP-2-induced ALP activity, Alizarin red mineralization, and COL1A1/OCN/OPN expression, whereas IL-6 had no effect. This identifies TNF-α and IL-1β as the key mediators of inflammation-driven suppression of BMP-2 osteogenesis.

---

## Perspective

This study provides in vivo evidence that an exaggerated inflammatory environment decreases BMP-2/ACS-induced ectopic bone mass through two coupled mechanisms — suppression of osteoblastogenesis-mediated bone formation and activation of osteoclastogenesis-mediated bone resorption. It offers a mechanistic explanation for the transient and unexpectedly low osteoinductive efficacy of BMP-2 observed in clinical spinal-fusion and fracture cases.

Because ectopic ossification here is driven by a potent osteogenic factor rather than being prevented, the work is directly relevant to the vault's ossification theme: it dissects how the inflammatory milieu tips the osteoblast/osteoclast balance that governs whether bone forms at an implant site. The identification of TNF-α and IL-1β (but not IL-6) as the operative cytokines suggests targeted anti-inflammatory strategies rather than broad immunosuppression.

Clinically, the authors argue that suppressing the inflammatory response around the peri-implantation period could enhance BMP-2 osteoinductive efficacy. Limitations include the artificial LPS model of systemic inflammation and the rat ectopic (non-orthotopic) site, which may not fully replicate the local inflammatory dynamics of a human spinal-fusion bed.
