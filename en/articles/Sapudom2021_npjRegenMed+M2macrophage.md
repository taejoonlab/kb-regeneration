# 3D in vitro M2 macrophage model to mimic modulation of tissue repair

## Citation (NLM)
Sapudom J, Karaman S, Mohamed WKE, Garcia-Sabaté A, Quartey BC, Teo JCM. 3D in vitro M2 macrophage model to mimic modulation of tissue repair. npj Regen Med. 2021;6:83. doi:10.1038/s41536-021-00193-5

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1038/s41536-021-00193-5](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41536-021-00193-5)

---

## Background

Wound healing / tissue repair proceeds through overlapping phases (homeostasis, inflammation, proliferation, remodeling) that are tightly orchestrated by cytokines, growth factors and cell–matrix interactions. Fibroblasts migrate into the wound, differentiate into contractile myofibroblasts via TGF-β1/Smad2/3 signaling, and deposit ECM (type I collagen, EDA-fibronectin). If myofibroblast activity persists, excessive ECM leads to scarring/fibrosis. Anti-inflammatory (M2) macrophages are dominant regulators of these transitions, and it has been hypothesized that the balance between regenerative healing and fibrosis pivots on the equilibrium between M2a and M2c subtypes, whose specific roles in controlling fibroblast fate remain poorly defined.

Most prior in vitro wound-healing models use 2D tissue-culture plastic, which lacks representative ECM, has non-physiological stiffness and no porosity. This study establishes a well-defined 3D collagen-matrix biomimetic model using THP-1-derived M2a-like and M2c-like macrophages to recapitulate the initiation and resolution phases of tissue repair, suitable for high-throughput and anti-fibrotic drug testing.

---

## Key Experiment Methods

1. **3D collagen matrix**: Rat-tail type I collagen reconstituted at 2 mg/ml (pore size ~8.1 µm, fibril diameter ~652 nm, elastic modulus ~85.5 Pa), covalently bound to glutaraldehyde-coated coverslips, with THP-1 cells embedded.
2. **Macrophage differentiation/activation**: THP-1 monocytes differentiated with PMA into uncommitted macrophages, then activated with IL-4/IL-13 (M^IL-4/IL-13, M2a-like) or IL-10 (M^IL-10, M2c-like).
3. **Phenotype characterization**: Flow-cytometry surface markers (CD14, CD68, CD80, CD86, CD105, CD163, CD206, HLA-DR; gMFI) and bead-based multiplex cytokine profiling of culture supernatants.
4. **Co-culture assays**: Primary human dermal fibroblasts or pre-differentiated (TGF-β1-treated) myofibroblasts co-cultured with each macrophage subtype for 3 days.
5. **Readouts**: αSMA, Coll1a1, EDA-FN by qPCR (RPS26 reference) and immunostaining; matrix remodeling (pore size, fibril diameter, elastic modulus) on decellularized matrices; cell migration (xz-confocal, migration depth). IL-10 neutralization with anti-IL-10 antibody to test causality.

---

## Results

- **Distinct M2 phenotypes**: M^IL-4/IL-13 showed higher CD105, CD206, HLA-DR and secreted more IFN-γ, IL-2, IL-4, IL-8, IL-12p70, MCP-1 and TGF-β1; M^IL-10 expressed high CD14, CD80, CD163 and secreted high IL-10 and IL-17. Profiles matched PBMC-derived M2a and M2c, respectively.
- **Initiation phase**: Only M^IL-4/IL-13 (M2a-like) drove fibroblast-to-myofibroblast differentiation, increasing αSMA, Coll1a1 and EDA-FN — even more strongly than direct 10 ng/ml TGF-β1 treatment, attributed to sustained paracrine TGF-β1 release. M^IL-10 co-cultures resembled untreated controls.
- **Matrix remodeling**: M^IL-4/IL-13 co-cultures produced smaller pore size, increased collagen fibril diameter and higher elastic modulus, consistent with early fibrosis.
- **Resolution phase**: M^IL-10 (M2c-like) dedifferentiated myofibroblasts back to fibroblasts (reduced αSMA, Coll1a1, EDA-FN; increased matrix porosity; lower elasticity). Anti-IL-10 antibody blocked this reversal, confirming IL-10 dependence.
- **Migration**: Myofibroblasts migrate less than fibroblasts; IL-10 / M^IL-10 restored myofibroblast migration, and anti-IL-10 abolished this — corroborating dedifferentiation.

---

## Perspective

The study provides a defined, reproducible 3D biomimetic platform that decouples the initiation (M2a/TGF-β1-driven myofibroblast differentiation and matrix stiffening) and resolution (M2c/IL-10-driven myofibroblast dedifferentiation) phases of tissue repair, emphasizing that co-culture (not fibroblast monoculture) is needed to model these dynamics. Using the THP-1 line overcomes donor-to-donor variability of primary cells and enables high-throughput anti-fibrotic drug screening. Limitations include reliance on a monocytic cell line rather than primary macrophages, a dermal-fibroblast context, and the simplified two-cell system that omits other wound cell types (endothelial cells, keratinocytes, adaptive immune cells). Since macrophage–fibroblast crosstalk and fibrotic scarring are central barriers to regeneration, the framework is of broader relevance for testing pro-regenerative and anti-scarring interventions.
