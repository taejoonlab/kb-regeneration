# Tenogenic Induction From Induced Pluripotent Stem Cells Unveils the Trajectory Towards Tenocyte Differentiation

## Citation (NLM)
Yoshimoto Y, Uezumi A, Ikemoto-Uezumi M, Tanaka K, Yu X, Kurosawa T, Yambe S, Maehara K, Ohkawa Y, Sotomaru Y, Shukunami C. Tenogenic Induction From Induced Pluripotent Stem Cells Unveils the Trajectory Towards Tenocyte Differentiation. Front Cell Dev Biol. 2022;10:780038. doi:10.3389/fcell.2022.780038

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.3389/fcell.2022.780038](https://doi.org/10.3389/fcell.2022.780038)

---

## Background
Tendons and ligaments are dense fibrous connective tissues critical for musculoskeletal function, with tenocytes as the major cell population characterized by scleraxis (Scx) expression. TGF-β signaling is essential for tendon development, but detailed molecular mechanisms controlling tenogenic differentiation remain unclear. Existing in vitro tenocyte culture systems suffer from dedifferentiation upon expansion, and iPSC-based tenogenic differentiation methods are either complicated or yield poor tendon marker expression. This study establishes a simple and efficient tenogenic differentiation system from iPSCs using ScxGFP reporter monitoring and elucidates the differentiation trajectory and regulatory mechanisms using single-cell RNA sequencing.

## Key Experiment Methods
1. Generation of ScxGFP iPSCs from ScxGFP transgenic mouse embryonic fibroblasts using Sendai virus reprogramming vectors
2. Stepwise monolayer differentiation protocol: mesodermal induction using CHIR99021 (Wnt agonist) + cyclopamine (Hedgehog inhibitor), followed by tenogenic induction with TGF-β isoforms
3. Comparison of TGF-β1, TGF-β2, and TGF-β3 for tenogenic induction efficiency using GFP fluorescence and tendon marker gene expression
4. Single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) of 4,592 cells across differentiation time points (T0, T3, T6, T9) using modified CEL-Seq2 protocol
5. PHATE and UMAP trajectory analysis to map tenogenic differentiation progression and identify cell clusters
6. Dose-dependent analysis of TGF-β2 effects on chondrogenic (Alcian blue staining) vs. tenogenic (GFP/Tnmd staining) differentiation
7. TGF-β signaling inhibition using SB431542 to test the inverse relationship between tenogenesis and chondrogenesis
8. Retinoic acid (ATRA) and RA antagonist (BMS493) treatment to investigate RA signaling effects on tenogenic differentiation

## Results
- ScxGFP iPSCs faithfully contributed to tendon/ligament lineage in teratomas and chimeric embryos, validating the GFP reporter system for tenogenic differentiation
- TGF-β2 was the most potent inducer of tenogenic differentiation among TGF-β isoforms, inducing significantly higher EGFP, Tnmd, and Scx expression compared to TGF-β1 or TGF-β3
- TGF-β2 treatment showed an inverse relationship with chondrogenesis: increasing TGF-β2 concentration decreased Alcian blue-positive cartilaginous areas while increasing GFP+ and Tnmd+ tenogenic areas
- TGF-β signaling inhibition with SB431542 completely suppressed tenogenesis and induced chondrogenic differentiation, confirming TGF-β signaling directs the tenogenic vs. chondrogenic fate decision
- scRNA-seq identified 11 distinct clusters including pluripotent cells (cluster 9), endothelial cells (cluster 10), early tenogenic progenitors (cluster 1, Aldh1a2+/Acta2+), differentiating tenocytes (clusters 2-3, Scx+), and mature tenocytes (cluster 6, Tnmd+)
- PHATE trajectory analysis revealed a continuous differentiation path from iPSCs through mesodermal progenitors to mature tenocytes, recapitulating in vivo developmental processes
- Retinoic acid (RA) signaling negatively regulated Scx+ cell induction; RA treatment suppressed tenogenic differentiation while RA antagonist enhanced it
- FACS analysis at T13 showed significant increases in Scx+, Scx+/Tnmd+, and Tnmd+ cell populations compared to T0

## Perspective
This study establishes a streamlined TGF-β2-driven tenogenic differentiation system from iPSCs that generates mature tenocytes expressing high levels of tendon markers, surpassing previous methods in simplicity and efficiency. The inverse relationship between tenogenesis and chondrogenesis regulated by TGF-β signaling reveals a fate decision mechanism where TGF-β promotes tenogenic differentiation while suppressing chondrogenic conversion. The scRNA-seq atlas provides a comprehensive view of the tenogenic differentiation trajectory, identifying key transitional populations. The discovery that RA signaling inhibits tenogenic differentiation reveals a new regulatory pathway, suggesting that RA modulation could be used to enhance tenocyte production. These findings provide a foundation for tendon/ligament tissue engineering and offer insights into the developmental biology of tenogenic lineage specification. Future work should address the heterogeneity of induced tenocytes and optimize conditions for generating homogeneous mature tenocyte populations for therapeutic applications.
