# Current perspectives on the multiple roles of osteoclasts: Mechanisms of osteoclast–osteoblast communication and potential clinical implications

## Citation (NLM)
Daponte V, Henke K, Drissi H. Current perspectives on the multiple roles of osteoclasts: Mechanisms of osteoclast–osteoblast communication and potential clinical implications. eLife. 2024;13:e95083. doi:10.7554/eLife.95083

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.7554/eLife.95083](https://doi.org/10.7554/eLife.95083)

---

## Background
Bone remodeling is a complex process involving the coordinated actions of osteoblasts and osteoclasts to maintain bone homeostasis. While the influence of osteoblasts on osteoclast differentiation is well established, the reciprocal regulation of osteoblasts by osteoclasts has long remained enigmatic. In recent years, a fascinating new role for osteoclasts has been unveiled in promoting bone formation and facilitating osteoblast migration to remodeling sites through multiple mechanisms, including the release of factors from the bone matrix following bone resorption and direct cell–cell interactions. Additionally, considerable evidence has shown that osteoclasts can secrete coupling factors known as clastokines, emphasizing their crucial role in maintaining bone homeostasis.

## Key Experiment Methods
1. Alizarin Red staining and Alkaline Phosphatase activity evaluation in osteoblast cultures treated with osteoclast conditioned media
2. Bone parameter analysis in osteoclast-specific conditional knockout mouse models
3. Intravital two-photon microscopy to observe direct osteoblast-osteoclast contact areas
4. EPHB4 overexpressing transgenic mice for bone density analysis
5. Conditional deletion of CTHRC1 in Ctsk-positive cells
6. C3aR antagonist/agonist administration in ovariectomized mouse models

## Results
1. TGF-β1 released during bone resorption induces bone marrow MSC migration to remodeling sites via SMAD signaling and stimulates WNT10B, CXCL16, and LIF expression in osteoclasts
2. IGF-1 released from the bone matrix activates the mTOR signaling pathway to induce osteoblastic differentiation of MSCs
3. EFNB2-EPHB4 interaction mediates bidirectional signaling; reverse signaling suppresses osteoclastogenesis while forward signaling promotes osteoblast differentiation
4. S1P is produced by osteoclasts via SPHK1 and secreted through SPNS2, binding to S1PR3 on osteoblasts to promote bone formation
5. CTHRC1 is secreted by mature osteoclasts and promotes osteoblast differentiation through WAIF1 and ROR2 receptors activating the PKCδ/ERK pathway
6. Osteoclast-derived C3a binds C3aR to promote osteoblast differentiation; C3aR antagonist inhibits bone formation and exacerbates bone loss in ovariectomized models
7. Osteoclast-derived WNT5A acquires its function through post-translational modification (serine phosphorylation) and contributes to bone mass maintenance
8. SLIT3 promotes osteoblast migration and proliferation via ROBO1/2 receptors and autocrinely inhibits osteoclastogenesis by suppressing Rac1 GTPase in osteoclasts

## Perspective
Osteoclasts play a critical role as active regulators of bone formation beyond their conventional function as bone-resorbing cells. Clastokines represent promising therapeutic targets for bone diseases, but more research is needed to fully decipher their mechanisms and pathways. Key unresolved issues include the controversy over the true cellular source of CTHRC1 (osteoclasts vs. osteoblasts/osteocytes), inconsistencies between SLIT3 knockout studies, and the cell-type-specific post-translational modification mechanism of WNT5A. Developing clastokine-based therapies will require elucidating their precise in vivo mechanisms and spatiotemporal occurrence during bone remodeling.
