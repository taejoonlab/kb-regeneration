# Differential chondrogenic differentiation between iPSC derived from healthy and OA cartilage is associated with changes in epigenetic regulation and metabolic transcriptomic signatures

## Citation (NLM)
Khan NM, Diaz-Hernandez ME, Chihab S, Priyadarshani P, Bhattaram P, Mortensen LJ, Guzzo RM, Drissi H. Differential chondrogenic differentiation between iPSC derived from healthy and OA cartilage is associated with changes in epigenetic regulation and metabolic transcriptomic signatures. eLife. 2023;12:e83138. doi:10.7554/eLife.83138

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.7554/eLife.83138](https://doi.org/10.7554/eLife.83138)

---

## Background

Induced pluripotent stem cells (iPSCs) are a promising cell source for regenerative medicine, but iPSCs are known to exhibit differences in differentiation capacity depending on the donor cell type (epigenetic memory). However, the differences in chondrogenic differentiation capacity between iPSCs derived from healthy cartilage and osteoarthritis (OA) cartilage, as well as the underlying molecular mechanisms, have not been elucidated. This study aimed to compare the chondrogenic differentiation potential of iPSCs derived from healthy articular chondrocytes (AC-iPSC) and OA chondrocytes (OA-iPSC) and to investigate the effect of OA disease state on iPSC differentiation capacity.

---

## Key Experiment Methods

- **iPSC generation**: iPSCs generated from chondrocytes isolated from healthy articular cartilage and OA articular cartilage via viral reprogramming (Oct4, Sox2, Klf4, c-Myc). AC-iPSC (clones #7, #14, #15), OA-iPSC (clones #2, #5, #8).
- **Mesenchymal stem cell (MSC) differentiation**: Direct plating method for iPSC→iMSC induction. Confirmed stellate morphology and downregulation of stemness genes.
- **Chondrogenic differentiation**: iMSCs cultured as micromass pellets with TGF-β3 treatment. Chondrogenesis induced for 21 days.
- **Pan-transcriptome analysis**: RNA-seq before and after chondrogenic differentiation of AC-iMSC vs OA-iMSC. DEG, GO enrichment, KEGG pathway analysis.
- **Epigenetic analysis**: Histone modification analysis (H3K4me3, H3K27ac, H3K27me3) and DNA methylation analysis.
- **Metabolomic analysis**: Comparison of gene expression related to cellular energy metabolism pathways (glycolysis, TCA cycle, oxidative phosphorylation).
- **Histochemistry**: Safranin O (GAG), collagen type II immunostaining, ALP staining (stem cell marker).

---

## Results

### iPSC characterization
- Both AC-iPSCs and OA-iPSCs were ALP positive, expressed SOX2/OCT4/NANOG/KLF4, and expressed TRA-1-60/SSEA-4 surface antigens → normal stem cell characteristics confirmed.
- Similar levels of stem cell marker expression between the two iPSC types.

### iMSC characteristics
- Both AC-iMSCs and OA-iMSCs exhibited spindle-shaped morphology, downregulation of SOX2/OCT4, and expression of MSC markers (CD44, CD73, CD90, CD105). No morphological or phenotypic differences between groups.

### Differences in chondrogenic differentiation capacity
- **AC-iPSC-derived chondrocytes**: Significantly higher chondrogenic capacity compared to OA-iPSC-derived chondrocytes.
  - AC-iMSC: increased expression of cartilage genes including COL2A1, ACAN, SOX9.
  - Safranin O staining intensity and GAG content: AC-iMSC > OA-iMSC.
- **OA-iPSC-derived chondrocytes**: Increased expression of hypertrophic markers (COL10A1), MMP13, ADAMTS5, and other catabolic genes → OA-like phenotype.

### Pan-transcriptome analysis
- Pathways increased in AC-iMSC-derived chondrocytes: energy metabolism (glycolysis, TCA cycle, oxidative phosphorylation), epigenetic regulation (histone modification, DNA methylation), collagen formation.
- Pathways increased in OA-iMSC-derived chondrocytes: ECM degradation, inflammatory response, apoptosis.
- Among DEGs, genes upregulated in AC-iMSCs were related to energy metabolism and epigenetics.

### Epigenetic and metabolic memory
- Epigenetic changes observed in OA chondrocytes (DNA methylation patterns, histone modifications) were maintained at the iMSC stage even after reprogramming.
- Reduced expression of genes related to mitochondrial function and oxidative phosphorylation in OA-iMSCs → presence of metabolic memory.
- These epigenetic and metabolic memories may underlie the reduced chondrogenic differentiation capacity of OA-iPSCs.

---

## Perspective

- This study provides the first evidence that iPSCs retain molecular and epigenetic memory based on the donor cell origin (healthy or OA disease state), which directly affects iPSC chondrogenic differentiation capacity.
- Healthy cartilage-derived iPSCs (AC-iPSCs) exhibit significantly higher chondrogenic potential than OA-derived iPSCs (OA-iPSCs), associated with differences in energy metabolism and epigenetic regulatory pathways.
- OA-iPSCs maintain molecular characteristics of OA chondrocytes, making them useful for OA modeling, but AC-iPSCs are more suitable for cartilage regeneration therapy.
- Strategies targeting epigenetic regulators and energy metabolism pathways may be effective in improving iPSC chondrogenic differentiation capacity.
- Future directions: (1) development of methods to eliminate iPSC epigenetic memory, (2) large-scale preclinical studies for application of AC-iPSCs in cartilage regeneration therapy, (3) establishment of OA disease modeling and drug screening platforms using OA-iPSCs.
