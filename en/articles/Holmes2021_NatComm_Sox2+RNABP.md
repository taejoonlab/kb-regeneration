# The Sox2 transcription factor binds RNA

## Citation (NLM)
Holmes ZE, Hamilton DJ, Hwang T, Parsonnet NV, Rinn JL, Wuttke DS, Batey RT. The Sox2 transcription factor binds RNA. Nat Commun. 2020;11:1805. doi:10.1038/s41467-020-15571-8

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-020-15571-8](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-020-15571-8)

---

## Background
An expanding set of transcription factors have been reported to associate with RNA to help regulate gene expression, but most studies do not distinguish direct from indirect (protein-mediated) association, and little is known about the biochemical basis of these interactions. Sox2 is a transcription factor critical for maintenance of pluripotency in embryonic stem cells and for neurogenesis, where it keeps neural progenitor cells (NPCs) in an undifferentiated, slowly proliferating state by repressing pro-neuronal differentiation proteins. Sox2 had been found associated with several lncRNAs (ES1, ES2, RMST) implicated in pluripotency and neuronal differentiation, but whether these interactions were direct was unknown. This study tested whether Sox2 binds RNA directly, both in vitro and in vivo.

---

## Key Experiment Methods
1. Fluorescence anisotropy (FA) and electrophoretic mobility shift assays (EMSA) to measure binding affinity of full-length Sox2 and its isolated HMG domain (aa 40-123) to DNA (FGF4 enhancer), the ES2 lncRNA (354 nt), and control nucleic acids.
2. Unbiased minimization/deletion analysis of ES2 into overlapping and non-overlapping segments, plus designed RNA hairpins (Loops A-C) to map binding determinants.
3. Systematic RNA mutagenesis (paired-region length, internal-loop-to-duplex conversion, hairpin truncation, terminal-loop removal) and testing of structured RNAs (riboswitches, tRNALeu) and DNA/RNA hybrid duplexes.
4. Equilibrium competition binding to test whether DNA and RNA binding are mutually exclusive; salt-dependence analysis to dissect electrostatic vs non-electrostatic contributions.
5. 1H-15N HSQC NMR of 15N-labeled Sox2-HMG in free, DNA-bound, and RNA-bound states.
6. Comprehensive alanine scan (48 of 84 HMG residues) mapping thermodynamic contributions to DNA vs RNA binding.
7. In vivo UV-crosslinking RIP-seq (UV-RIP) and formaldehyde RIP (fRIP) in mouse embryonic stem cells (mESC), with UMIs and 4 biological replicates each.

---

## Results
- Sox2 binds the ES2 lncRNA directly with high affinity (KD,app ~18 nM), modestly weaker than consensus DNA (~2 nM) but far tighter than ssDNA/non-consensus DNA (>200-fold difference).
- The isolated HMG domain fully recapitulated binding, identifying it as the RNA-binding domain.
- Sox2-HMG bound all ES2 fragments and derived hairpins equivalently, indicating no single sequence-specific site; it preferentially recognizes A-form double-stranded RNA, with internal/terminal loops playing minor roles.
- Sox2-HMG bound structured bacterial RNAs (riboswitches, tRNALeu) and a DNA/RNA hybrid duplex with comparable affinity, arguing for broad A-form nucleic-acid recognition.
- DNA and RNA binding are mutually exclusive (overlapping surface); both form 1:1 and weaker 2:1 complexes; RNA and specific DNA binding are similarly electrostatically driven (~8-9 counterions released).
- NMR showed Sox2-HMG is only partially ordered upon RNA binding, versus a full disorder-to-order transition with specific DNA, indicating distinct engagement modes.
- Alanine scanning: base-specific DNA-contact residues (Asn46, Arg43, Tyr110, Met49) are dispensable for RNA binding, while electrostatic surface residues contribute more to RNA; major-wing core residues (Phe48, Trp51, Trp79) matter for both.
- In vivo, UV-RIP identified 54 directly crosslinked Sox2-RNA interactions in mESC, and fRIP identified over a thousand (~75% of UV-RIP hits recovered), confirming Sox2 binds RNA in living cells.

---

## Perspective
The work establishes that Sox2, beyond its role as a pioneer transcription factor, is a bona fide RNA-binding protein that uses its HMG domain to engage RNA with a high-affinity/low-specificity paradigm, preferring A-form double-stranded geometry. Because Sox2 is a master regulator of pluripotency and of neural progenitor maintenance/neurogenesis, this RNA-binding activity could contribute to how Sox2 interfaces with the lncRNAs (e.g., RMST) that govern neuronal differentiation, expanding the mechanistic repertoire of a factor central to neural stem/progenitor biology. Limitations include the largely in vitro biochemical/biophysical focus, use of mESCs rather than neural cells for the in vivo interactome, mapping of mutational data onto the DNA-bound structure (an approximation given the different NMR behavior), and the fact that the functional/regulatory consequences of Sox2-RNA binding in cells were not directly tested.
