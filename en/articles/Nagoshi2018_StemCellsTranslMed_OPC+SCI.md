# Human Spinal Oligodendrogenic Neural Progenitor Cells Promote Functional Recovery After Spinal Cord Injury by Axonal Remyelination and Tissue Sparing

## Citation (NLM)
Nagoshi N, Khazaei M, Ahlfors JE, Ahuja CS, Nori S, Wang J, Shibata S, Fehlings MG. Human Spinal Oligodendrogenic Neural Progenitor Cells Promote Functional Recovery After Spinal Cord Injury by Axonal Remyelination and Tissue Sparing. Stem Cells Transl Med. 2018;7(11):806-818. doi:10.1002/sctm.17-0269

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1002/sctm.17-0269](https://doi.org/10.1002/sctm.17-0269)

---

## Background

Cell transplantation using neural precursor cells (NPCs) is an attractive strategy for traumatic spinal cord injury (SCI), aiming to replace lost cells, remyelinate denuded host axons, and promote tissue sparing. Oligodendrocytes are particularly vulnerable to the cytotoxic acute lesion environment, and their loss leads to demyelination and failure of saltatory conduction. However, when typical NPCs are transplanted, the proportion differentiating into mature oligodendrocytes is very low (in the post-injury niche most NPCs become astrocytes, ~80%, associated with increased allodynia), limiting remyelination.

To overcome this, the authors developed a method to bias directly reprogrammed human NPCs (drNPCs) toward an oligodendrogenic fate (oNPCs) while preserving tripotency (ability to make neurons, astrocytes, and oligodendrocytes). drNPCs are generated from bone marrow somatic cells without passing through a pluripotent state, making them potentially safer (lower tumorigenicity) than iPSC-derived grafts and suitable for autologous transplantation. This is the first study to evaluate human drNPCs biased toward an oligodendrogenic fate for SCI repair.

---

## Key Experiment Methods

1. **Generation of oNPCs**: Four human NPC lines tested (fetal cortical, fetal spinal, iPSC-derived, drNPCs). Rostral NPCs caudalized with retinoic acid (RA, 9 days), ventralized with Shh agonist purmorphamine, then treated with PDGF-AA/thyroxine to bias toward oligodendrogenic fate; protocol mimics neural tube patterning in vitro.
2. **Molecular characterization**: RT-PCR for rostral/caudal identity markers (Otx2, FoxG1, HoxA4) and oligodendrogenic transcription factors (Nkx2.2, Olig2, Nkx6.1); hierarchical clustering.
3. **In vitro differentiation**: Growth factor withdrawal + 0.1% FBS; immunocytochemistry for Nestin, Pax6, Sox2, βIII-tubulin (neurons), GFAP (astrocytes), O1 (oligodendrocytes).
4. **In vivo SCI model**: Clip-compression (23 g, 1 min) at T7 in immunodeficient athymic RNU rats; oNPCs, control unpatterned drNPCs, or vehicle (aCSF) transplanted 9 days post-injury (4×10^5 cells).
5. **Histology and immunohistochemistry**: Stereology for cell survival (HuN+), proliferation (Ki67+), tri-lineage differentiation (βIII-tubulin, GFAP, APC), oligodendrocyte markers (Olig2, GST-pi, MBP), luxol fast blue/H&E for white matter and lesion morphometry.
6. **Immuno-electron microscopy**: Nanogold-labeled Stem121+ to distinguish graft-derived vs endogenous myelin; g-ratio analysis.
7. **Behavioral assays**: BBB open-field locomotion, CatWalk gait analysis (stride length, swing speed), tail-flick test for thermal allodynia.
8. **Tumorigenicity/safety**: oNPCs or control drNPCs transplanted into intact spinal cords of NOD/SCID mice, observed up to 150 days.

---

## Results

- Human NPCs (including drNPCs) were successfully biased toward an oligodendrogenic fate; oNPCs upregulated Nkx2.2, Olig2, and Nkx6.1 while retaining progenitor markers (Pax6, Sox2, Nestin).
- In vitro, oNPCs generated significantly more oligodendrocytes (O1+: 30.2% vs 7.4% for unpatterned NPCs) and fewer astrocytes (GFAP+: 24.0% vs 40.1%), preserving neuron generation (~19%), confirming maintained tripotency.
- In vivo after SCI, transplanted oNPCs survived (~24% of input), migrated rostrocaudally, and showed low proliferation (Ki67+: 2.5%). oNPCs produced far more oligodendrocytes (APC+/GFAP−: 47.7% vs 13.6% for control NPCs).
- oNPCs expressed MBP closely associated with host NF200+ axons; electron microscopy showed graft-derived immature myelin sheaths (nanogold-labeled) and preserved endogenous myelin; g-ratio regression coefficient significantly smaller and myelinated axon counts higher in oNPC group.
- Histomorphometry showed significantly larger white matter area and reduced lesion area at the epicenter in the oNPC group (no difference in gray matter or cavity).
- Functionally, oNPC rats showed significantly improved BBB scores (weeks 7-9) and better CatWalk stride length and swing speed, with no increase in thermal allodynia (tail-flick).
- Safety: after 150 days in intact NOD/SCID mouse spinal cords, no tumor formation, no neural rosettes; Ki67+ ~0.94% of graft cells.

---

## Perspective

This study demonstrates that biasing tripotent human drNPCs toward an oligodendrogenic fate before transplantation substantially increases oligodendrocyte output, promotes axonal remyelination and white matter/tissue sparing, and improves motor recovery without inducing allodynia or tumors in a clinically relevant rat clip-compression SCI model. The use of drNPCs (derived from a patient's own bone marrow somatic cells, without a pluripotent intermediate) offers advantages in safety, autologous compatibility, and shorter reprogramming time relative to iPSC-derived cells.

Limitations acknowledged by the authors: cells were transplanted in the subacute (not chronic) phase, while many patients present chronically; only a thoracic model and a single parent hNPC line were tested; longer follow-up and large-animal studies, plus genomic/epigenomic stability assessment, are needed before clinical translation. Future directions include chronic-phase and cervical models, testing across multiple cell sources, and optimizing induction/culture protocols toward clinical application.
