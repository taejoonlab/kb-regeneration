# Sequentially induced motor neurons from human fibroblasts facilitate locomotor recovery in a rodent spinal cord injury model

## Citation (NLM)
Lee H, Lee HY, Lee BE, Gerovska D, Park SY, Zaehres H, Araúzo-Bravo MJ, Kim JI, Ha Y, Schöler HR, Kim JB. Sequentially induced motor neurons from human fibroblasts facilitate locomotor recovery in a rodent spinal cord injury model. eLife. 2020;9:e52069. doi:10.7554/eLife.52069

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.7554/eLife.52069](https://doi.org/10.7554/eLife.52069)

---

## Background

Spinal cord injury (SCI) causes devastating loss of sensory/motor function with no fully restorative treatment available. Cell replacement therapy is a promising intervention, and transplantation of neural cells derived from pluripotent stem cells (PSCs) has shown efficacy in SCI animal models. However, ESCs raise ethical issues and iPSCs carry tumorigenic potential, impeding clinical translation. Direct conversion of somatic cells bypasses the pluripotent state, but existing protocols yield heterogeneous, low-yield, post-mitotic (non-scalable) cells requiring multiple transcription factors, so the therapeutic effects of directly converted neurons had not been demonstrated.

The authors hypothesized that POU5F1(OCT4), a versatile reprogramming factor that binds the MN specification factor ISL1, could drive fibroblasts toward a motor neuron fate, and that adding LHX3 would complete the conversion. The goal was scalable production of pure, functional human induced motor neurons (iMNs) suitable for personalized cell therapy of SCI.

---

## Key Experiment Methods

1. Sequential transduction of human adult fibroblasts (HF1, HF2) with POU5F1(OCT4) first, then LHX3, in defined neural induction and MN induction media (with SHH, IGF1, BDNF, GDNF, CNTF, NT3, cAMP).
2. Establishment and characterization of self-renewing iMN-intermediate cells (iMNICs); marker analysis (ISL1, HB9, NKX6.1, LHX3, SOX2, PAX6, OLIG2) by qRT-PCR and immunocytochemistry; proliferation/doubling-time and passage (P2 vs P13) assays.
3. Screening of MN specification factors (PAX6, NKX6.1, OLIG2, LIM1, LHX3) and adherent vs sphere culture optimization; conversion efficiency quantified by TUJ1+ and HB9+TUJ1+ counts.
4. HB9:GFP reporter lentivirus for live tracking; immunostaining for MN markers (NKX6.1, ISL1, HB9, CHAT, TUJ1, MAP2) and synaptic markers (SYN1, SV2).
5. Whole-cell patch-clamp electrophysiology (action potentials, TTX-sensitive Na+ currents).
6. Neuromuscular junction (NMJ) assay by co-culture with C2C12 myotubes (AChR, α-bungarotoxin, curare block).
7. Global RNA-seq comparison of iMNICs/iMNs vs fibroblasts, fetal NPCs, ESC/iPSC-derived wild-type MNs, and fetal spinal cord (heatmap, hierarchical clustering, PCA, HOX gene analysis).
8. Transplantation of HB9:GFP+ iMNs into a rat T9 compressive SCI model; engraftment (TUJ1, MBP), cavity size (H&E), BBB locomotor scoring; tumorigenicity test in nude mice.

---

## Results

- Sequential POU5F1(OCT4)→LHX3 induction converted human fibroblasts into HB9+TUJ1+ iMNs with 70–90% efficiency; POU5F1(OCT4) alone upregulated only ISL1 and was insufficient for mature MNs, while adding LHX3 (but not other factors) completed conversion, best in sphere culture.
- iMNICs are a self-renewing, proliferative intermediate distinct from neural progenitors (negative for SOX1, PAX6, SOX2, NCAM, OLIG2; no pluripotent state), maintained >13 passages with stable doubling time (~28–29 h), enabling large-scale production of pure iMNs.
- iMNs showed bona fide MN identity: MN marker co-expression, minimal dopaminergic (TH+) or astrocytic (GFAP+) contamination, and transgene silencing.
- iMNs were electrophysiologically mature (60% fired action potentials; TTX-sensitive Na+ currents) and formed functional NMJs driving curare-sensitive rhythmic myotube contraction.
- RNA-seq: iMNICs/iMNs clustered tightly with wild-type MNs (and iMNICs with fetal spinal cord), expressing cervical/thoracic HOX genes (HOXC4-9, HOXA9, HOXD9) and MN genes (HB9, ISL1, NKX6.1, CHAT), distinct from NPCs and fibroblasts.
- Transplantation into rat SCI: iMNs engrafted (GFP+/TUJ1+, surrounded by host MBP+ oligodendrocytes), reduced lesion cavity, and significantly improved BBB locomotor scores from week 4 through week 8 (iMN n=10 vs PBS n=6). No tumors formed over 12 months in nude mice.

---

## Perspective

This study provides the first demonstration of therapeutic efficacy of directly converted human motor neurons in an SCI model, overcoming the key scalability barrier through a self-renewing iMNIC intermediate generated with a minimal two-factor (OCT4, LHX3) sequential strategy. The transient proliferative intermediate—distinct from both pluripotent and neural progenitor states—addresses tumorigenicity and yield concerns simultaneously, offering a route to autologous, personalized cell therapy.

Limitations include the modest fraction of cells firing multiple/spontaneous action potentials, incomplete functional recovery, and the need for longer-term and larger-animal safety/efficacy validation before clinical translation. The scalable iMN platform also enables MN subtype-specific disease modeling and cell-based drug screening for motor neuron disorders. Future work should refine subtype specification (rostro-caudal/columnar identity via HOX patterning) and characterize host integration and circuit reconstruction after transplantation.
