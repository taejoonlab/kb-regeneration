# A new neutrophil subset promotes CNS neuron survival and axon regeneration

## Citation (NLM)
Sas AR, Carbajal KS, Jerome AD, Menon R, Yoon C, Kalinski AL, Giger RJ, Segal BM. A new neutrophil subset promotes CNS neuron survival and axon regeneration. Nat Immunol. 2020;21(12):1496-1505. doi:10.1038/s41590-020-00813-0

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1038/s41590-020-00813-0](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41590-020-00813-0)

---

## Background

Transected axons typically fail to regenerate in the central nervous system (CNS), producing chronic disability after traumatic brain or spinal cord injury, glaucoma, multiple sclerosis and ischemia–reperfusion injury of the eye. Although neuroinflammation is often viewed as detrimental, there is growing evidence that alternatively activated, reparative leukocyte subsets and their products can improve neurological outcomes. A classic model of immune-driven CNS axon regeneration is optic nerve crush (ONC) injury combined with intraocular (i.o.) injection of zymosan, a fungal cell wall extract whose active ingredient β-1,3-glucan signals through the innate receptor dectin-1. The cellular and molecular mediators triggering neurorepair downstream of dectin-1 signaling were poorly understood.

This study identifies a unique granulocyte with characteristics of an immature neutrophil that accumulates in the vitreous in response to i.o. zymosan and exerts neuroprotective and axonogenic effects, in part via secretion of growth factors. The same myeloid subset also drove axon regeneration in the spinal cord, demonstrating relevance across CNS compartments.

---

## Key Experiment Methods

1. Optic nerve crush (ONC) injury in mice with i.o. injection of zymosan or PBS, combined with systemic (i.p.) administration of anti-CXCR2 antiserum or normal rabbit serum (NRS) control.
2. Flow cytometric analysis of vitreal infiltrates over time (surface markers Ly6G, CD14, Ly6B, CD101, MPO, elastase); cytospins with Wright-Giemsa staining for nuclear morphology.
3. Single-cell RNA sequencing (10x Genomics) of purified i.o. Ly6G+ cells from NRS and anti-CXCR2 groups; RT-qPCR for Arg1, Il4ra, Mrc1, NGF, IGF-1.
4. Assessment of axon regeneration by GAP-43 immunohistochemistry of longitudinal optic nerve sections; RGC survival by BRN3a+ counts in retinal whole mounts.
5. Adoptive transfer of Ly6G+ cells purified from peritoneal lavage 4 h vs 3 d after i.p. zymosan (4 h Nϕ vs 3 d Nϕ) into eyes of ONC-injured mice; use of Rag1−/−, dectin-1/TLR2-deficient and Ccr2−/− hosts.
6. In vitro neurite outgrowth assays with primary RGCs and dorsal root ganglion (DRG) neurons co-cultured with neutrophils or conditioned medium (NCM); growth-factor neutralization (anti-NGF, anti-IGF-1) and ELISA/antibody array screening.
7. Traumatic SCI model: laceration of spinal cord dorsal columns at T4, with sciatic nerve injection of neutrophil subsets 5 d prior; conditioning injury (sciatic nerve crush) as positive control; immunohistological analysis of axon regrowth at day 52.

---

## Results

- Myeloid cells (neutrophils, monocytes/macrophages) dominated vitreal infiltrates after i.o. zymosan. Unexpectedly, systemic anti-CXCR2 enhanced RGC survival and axon regeneration; it delayed but did not prevent neutrophil accumulation and skewed the infiltrate toward an immature neutrophil phenotype (Ly6Glo CD14hi CD101lo–neg, ring-shaped nuclei).
- scRNA-seq resolved immature (cluster 3), intermediate and mature clusters; the immature cluster was >3-fold enriched with anti-CXCR2 (36.8% vs 10.6%).
- Zymosan-induced Ly6Glo cells had characteristics of alternatively activated ("N2"/M2-like) cells, with elevated Arg1, Mrc1, Il4ra, Itga6, Tgfb1, Igf1 and Ccl5; arginase-1 protein was confirmed by flow cytometry.
- Adoptively transferred 3 d Nϕ (which mimic the pro-regenerative phenotype) induced robust RGC survival and axon regeneration, while 4 h Nϕ and naive bone marrow neutrophils were ineffective. Effect persisted in dectin-1/TLR2- and Ccr2-deficient hosts and directly stimulated RGC neurite outgrowth, indicating a monocyte-independent, direct action with a therapeutic window extending beyond the acute injury.
- Ly6Glo neutrophils secreted an array of growth factors; NGF and IGF-1 were highly elevated in conditioned medium. Neutralizing either (and especially both) reduced neurite outgrowth in vitro and axon regeneration in vivo; recombinant NGF + IGF-1 acted collaboratively.
- 3 d Nϕ also stimulated DRG neurite outgrowth and, in the traumatic SCI model, drove regrowth of severed dorsal column axons past the injury site, comparable to conditioning injury — establishing pro-regenerative function in the spinal cord as well as the optic nerve.

---

## Perspective

The study defines a previously uncharacterized immature, alternatively activated neutrophil subset as a driver of CNS neuroprotection and axon regeneration, acting in part through NGF and IGF-1 secretion, and shows this activity generalizes across CNS compartments (optic nerve and spinal cord). A human immature-neutrophil-like cell line reportedly showed similar capacity, suggesting translational potential toward neurorestorative immunotherapies. Limitations include reliance on the zymosan/ONC paradigm and pharmacological CXCR2 blockade to expand the subset, incomplete definition of the full secreted-factor repertoire, and the challenge of generating sufficient cells for therapeutic use. Future directions include delineating developmental pathways of these cells, testing in clinically relevant SCI settings, and exploring human cell sources.
