# Skeletal stem cells: insights into maintaining and regenerating the skeleton

## Citation (NLM)
Serowoky MA, Arata CE, Crump JG, Mariani FV. Skeletal stem cells: insights into maintaining and regenerating the skeleton. Development. 2020;147(10):dev179325. doi:10.1242/dev.179325

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1242/dev.179325](https://doi.org/10.1242/dev.179325)

---

## Background
Skeletal stem cells (SSCs) are responsible for growth, maintenance, and repair of the skeleton. Historically, SSCs were defined as bone marrow-derived cells with inconsistent characteristics. Recent in vivo lineage-tracing studies have revealed SSC populations not only in bone marrow but also in periosteum and growth plate resting zone, demonstrating high heterogeneity in lineage potential. Additionally, in some non-mammalian vertebrates and during digit tip regeneration, osteoblast dedifferentiation contributes to skeletal regeneration. Understanding the diversity, plasticity, and niche regulation of SSCs is critical for developing targeted cellular therapies for skeletal repair.

## Key Experiment Methods
1. Review and synthesis of genetic Cre-mediated lineage-tracing experiments in postnatal mouse models using various Cre/CreER drivers (Grem1, Lepr, Gli1, Axin2, Col2a1, PTHrP, Sox9, Ctsk, Prrx1, Cxcl12, Ebf3, Nestin, Mx1, Acta2)
2. Comparative analysis of SSC populations isolated by FACS using cell surface marker combinations (e.g., Itgav+/CD200+, CD164+/PDPN+/CD73+, Sca-1+/PDGFR-α+, CD146+)
3. Evaluation of SSC contributions to bone, cartilage, adipocyte, and stromal lineages through transplantation assays (subcutaneous, kidney capsule, intravenous)
4. Assessment of SSC requirements for bone formation and repair through diphtheria toxin-mediated ablation studies
5. Analysis of niche signaling pathways (Wnt, BMP, Hedgehog, PDGF) regulating SSC maintenance and differentiation
6. Review of osteoblast dedifferentiation mechanisms during zebrafish fin/skull regeneration and murine digit tip regeneration using time-lapse imaging and photoconversion techniques

## Results
- Multiple SSC populations exist across different anatomical locations with distinct temporal emergence and lineage potentials:
  - Bone marrow: Grem1+ cells (early postnatal, multilineage), Lepr+ cells (adult, perivascular, adipogenic/osteogenic), Ebf3+ cells (Lepr+ subset, self-renewing stromal)
  - Growth plate: PTHrP+ and Col2a1+ resting zone cells (postnatal onset ~P6, self-renewing chondroprogenitors contributing to osteoblasts via hypertrophic transition)
  - Periosteum: Ctsk+ cells (self-renewing, osteochondroadipogenic), Sox9+ cells (normally osteogenic, injury-induced chondrogenic), Gli1+ cells (transient juvenile population)
- SSC hierarchy is not a single apex system like hematopoiesis; rather, heterogeneous pools work together with overlapping and distinct functions
- Hypertrophic chondrocytes can escape apoptosis and transdifferentiate into osteoblasts and potentially marrow SSCs
- Osteoblast dedifferentiation occurs during zebrafish fin and skull regeneration (downregulation of bglap, upregulation of runx2b) and murine digit tip regeneration
- Niche signals regulate SSC behavior: Wnt promotes undifferentiated state, BMP signaling provides feedback for SSC expansion, Hedgehog promotes proliferation without premature differentiation
- During fracture repair, periosteum and bone marrow are primary sources of bone-forming cells; callus chondrocytes show hybrid osteochondral properties with co-expression of Sox9 and Runx2 at early stages

## Perspective
The SSC field has moved from a simplified bone marrow-centric view to recognizing multiple anatomically and functionally distinct SSC populations. Key challenges remain: defining lineage relationships between diverse SSC pools, understanding how developmental origins influence adult behavior, and elucidating niche factor regulation. The plasticity observed—both in terms of multiple SSC sources and osteoblast dedifferentiation—suggests the skeletal system has redundant repair mechanisms. Single-cell RNA sequencing and cellular barcoding technologies will be essential for creating a complete SSC catalog and lineage tree. Understanding SSC biology in aging and disease contexts (e.g., inflammation reducing SSC function) may lead to pharmacological rejuvenation strategies for improved fracture healing and treatment of skeletal degeneration.
