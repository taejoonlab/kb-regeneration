# Adipogenesis, Osteogenesis, and Chondrogenesis of Human Mesenchymal Stem/Stromal Cells: A Comparative Transcriptome Approach

## Citation (NLM)
Robert AW, Marcon BH, Dallagiovanna B, Shigunov P. Adipogenesis, osteogenesis, and chondrogenesis of human mesenchymal stem/stromal cells: a comparative transcriptome approach. Front Cell Dev Biol. 2020;8:561. doi:10.3389/fcell.2020.00561

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.3389/fcell.2020.00561](https://doi.org/10.3389/fcell.2020.00561)

## Background

Mesenchymal stem/stromal cells (MSCs) are adult stem cells with multilineage differentiation capacity, capable of differentiating into adipogenic, osteogenic, and chondrogenic lineages. MSC differentiation is a complex and precisely regulated process, and expression profiles of mRNA, microRNA, circular RNA, and long non-coding RNA have been studied using various high-throughput technologies (microarray, RNA-seq). Understanding the regulatory networks of MSC differentiation is essential for advancing regenerative medicine and tissue engineering. This review compares and synthesizes transcriptome analysis results from adipogenic, osteogenic, and chondrogenic differentiation processes, highlighting key molecules and major signaling pathways in each differentiation pathway.

This paper is a **review article** that summarizes transcriptome studies on MSC differentiation into adipogenic, osteogenic, and chondrogenic lineages:

- **Analysis targets**: mRNA, microRNA, circular RNA, lncRNA expression profiles.
- **Comparison items**: commonalities and differences among three differentiation lineages, temporal gene expression changes, key signaling pathways and transcription factors.

## Results

### MSC characteristics and markers
- ISCT criteria: CD105+/CD73+/CD90+(>95%), CD45−/CD34−/CD14−/CD19−/HLA-DR−(<2%).
- Tissue-specific MSC marker differences exist among bone marrow, adipose tissue, and umbilical cord blood.
- Immunophenotypic changes observed with increasing passage number.

### Adipogenic differentiation
- Key transcription factors: **PPARγ, C/EBPα, C/EBPβ, C/EBPδ**.
- Early stage: increased C/EBPβ/δ expression → activation of PPARγ and C/EBPα.
- Activated PPARγ induces expression of adipocyte-specific genes (adiponectin, FABP4, LPL).
- Major signals: insulin, IGF-1, glucocorticoid signaling. WNT signaling inhibition is required for adipogenic differentiation.
- MicroRNA: miR-143/145 promotes adipogenesis, miR-27b inhibits it.

### Osteogenic differentiation
- Key transcription factors: **RUNX2, Osterix (SP7)**.
- Early stage: increased RUNX2 expression → induction of Osterix → expression of osteoblast-specific genes (Col1a1, ALPL, BGLAP, SPP1).
- Major signals: BMP/Smad and WNT/β-catenin signaling are central. NOTCH, FGF, and Hedgehog signaling also involved.
- MicroRNA: miR-29b promotes osteogenesis (relieves targeting of Col1a1, RUNX2), miR-125b inhibits it.
- Adipogenic vs. osteogenic differentiation antagonism: competitive relationship between PPARγ and RUNX2. WNT signaling promotes osteogenesis and inhibits adipogenesis.

### Chondrogenic differentiation
- Key transcription factors: **SOX9, SOX5, SOX6**.
- SOX9 activates expression of cartilage-specific ECM genes including Col2a1 and Acan. SOX5/SOX6 cooperate with SOX9.
- Major signals: TGF-β (especially TGF-β3) is essential. BMP signaling is also important. FGF, WNT, and IHH-PTHrP signaling regulate chondrocyte maturation and hypertrophy.
- Hypoxic environment (HIF-1α) promotes chondrogenesis.
- MicroRNA: miR-140 (cartilage formation defects in miR-140−/− mice), miR-145 (targets SOX9, inhibits chondrogenesis), miR-199a, miR-221.
- LncRNA: HOTTIP, ROCR, etc., involved in chondrogenesis regulation.

### Comparison of three differentiation processes
- Commonalities: TGF-β/BMP, WNT, and FGF signaling are important in all three differentiation processes.
- Differences:
  - Adipogenesis: PPARγ is the master regulator. Cooperation with C/EBP family.
  - Osteogenesis: RUNX2/Osterix are master regulators. BMP/Smad signaling predominant.
  - Chondrogenesis: SOX9/SOX5/SOX6 are master regulators. TGF-β signaling dependent.
- Adipogenesis and osteogenesis are mutually antagonistic; chondrogenesis is relatively independently regulated.

### Insights from single-cell transcriptome studies
- Cell heterogeneity influences differentiation capacity of each lineage.
- Single-cell transcriptome analysis presents new directions for MSC differentiation research.

## Perspective

- This review provides an integrated comparison and analysis of transcriptome data from three major MSC differentiation pathways (adipogenesis, osteogenesis, chondrogenesis), presenting unique regulatory networks and common mechanisms of each differentiation process.
- Molecular understanding of MSC differentiation regulation is essential for developing strategies to improve differentiation efficiency in regenerative medicine.
- In particular, the antagonistic relationship between adipogenesis and osteogenesis is an important therapeutic target for bone metabolic diseases such as osteoporosis.
- Future challenges: (1) elucidating the functional significance of transcriptomic differences among various MSC sources (bone marrow, adipose, umbilical cord blood), (2) investigating MSC heterogeneity and fate decision mechanisms through single-cell transcriptome analysis, (3) functional validation of lncRNA and circular RNA in MSC differentiation regulation, (4) development of personalized MSC therapies utilizing transcriptomic information.
