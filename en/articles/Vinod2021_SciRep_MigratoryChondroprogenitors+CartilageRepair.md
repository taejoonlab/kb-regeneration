# Migratory chondroprogenitors retain superior intrinsic chondrogenic potential for regenerative cartilage repair as compared to human fibronectin derived chondroprogenitors

## Citation (NLM)

Vinod E, Johnson NN, Kumar S, Amirtham SM, James JV, Livingston A, Rebekah G, Daniel AJ, Ramasamy B, Sathishkumar S. Migratory chondroprogenitors retain superior intrinsic chondrogenic potential for regenerative cartilage repair as compared to human fibronectin derived chondroprogenitors. Sci Rep. 2021;11(1):23685. doi:10.1038/s41598-021-03082-5

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-021-03082-5](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-021-03082-5)

---

## Background

Cell-based cartilage repair predominantly uses mesenchymal stem cells (MSCs) and chondrocytes, but the resulting repair tissue is often a mix of hyaline and fibrocartilage, giving inferior long-term biomechanical and functional outcomes. Cartilage-derived chondroprogenitors have gained interest because they show a phenotypic predisposition toward chondrogenesis and reduced hypertrophic proclivity (lower Collagen type I and RUNX2) compared with chondrocytes and bone marrow MSCs. Two standard isolation routes yield distinct progenitor subsets: fibronectin adhesion assay-derived chondroprogenitors (FAA-CP) and migratory chondroprogenitors (MCP) that emerge from cartilage explants. Both meet ISCT minimal MSC criteria, but they had never been directly compared from the same human tissue. Because in vivo cartilage oxygen tension is far below the 21% used in standard culture (as low as ~1% near subchondral bone) and hypoxia is reported to favor chondrogenesis while limiting hypertrophy, this study compared human FAA-CPs and MCPs under normoxia and hypoxia to determine which subset holds superior potential for cartilage repair.

---

## Key Experiment Methods

**1. Chondroprogenitor isolation from human OA cartilage**
- Cartilage from three high-grade osteoarthritic (Kellgren-Lawrence 4) knee joints (age 55 ± 4 y) undergoing total knee replacement
- FAA-CP: enzymatic digestion (pronase then collagenase type II) → fibronectin adhesion assay (4000 cells/well, 20 min) → clonal expansion (>32 cells/clone) with TGFβ2 and FGF2
- MCP: explant outgrowth after collagenase, migrated cells harvested by day 10; expanded without added growth factors
- Four groups: FAA-CP and MCP, each under normoxia (21% O₂) or hypoxia (1% O₂)

**2. Growth and phenotype characterization (passage 1)**
- Cell diameter (automated counter) and cell cycle analysis by DAPI/flow cytometry
- FACS surface markers: positive MSC (CD105, CD73, CD90), negative (CD34, CD45, CD14); integrins (CD49e, CD29, CD49b); chondrogenesis-associated (CD166, CD146, Podoplanin); immunogenic (HLA-ABC, HLA-DR, CD80, CD86)

**3. Chondrogenic potential**
- qRT-PCR: chondrogenic SOX-9, ACAN, COL2A1; fibrocartilage COL1A1; hypertrophic COL10A1, RUNX2, MMP-13; COL2A1/COL1A1 ratio computed; normalized to GAPDH, ΔΔCt vs normoxia FAA-CP
- Trilineage differentiation (adipo/osteo/chondro) with Oil Red O, Alizarin Red, Alcian blue, Toluidine blue, Picrosirius red, and collagen type II immunofluorescence
- 3-week chondrogenic pellet cultures; biochemical total GAG/DNA (DMMB and PicoGreen)

---

## Results

**Comparable growth kinetics and MSC immunophenotype**
FAA-CPs showed clonal growth reaching a population doubling of 5 by day 12; MCPs migrated from explants by day 7 and expanded without exogenous growth factors. Passage-1 cells of all four groups shared spindle-shaped, honeycomb morphology and comparable cell diameter, with no significant cell-cycle differences. All groups met ISCT criteria (high CD105/CD73/CD90, low CD34/CD45/CD14). Normoxia MCP expressed significantly higher CD105 than both FAA-CP groups (p < 0.01), and MCPs had higher CD49b (p < 0.05) and notably higher CD146 (a marker of enhanced chondrogenesis) than FAA-CPs.

**Normoxia MCPs showed the strongest chondrogenic and weakest hypertrophic profile**
ACAN was expressed highest in normoxia MCP (significantly higher than normoxia FAA-CP, p = 0.027), with moderate SOX-9 and COL2A1 across groups. MCPs had markedly lower COL1A1 (fibrocartilage marker) and RUNX2 than FAA-CPs (COL1A1: normoxia MCP vs FAA-CPs p = 0.000; RUNX2: p = 0.024 and 0.009 for normoxia/hypoxia MCP). The functional COL2A1/COL1A1 ratio was significantly higher in normoxia MCP than in all other groups (p < 0.05).

**Hypoxia specifically lowered COL10A1**
Hypoxia reduced COL10A1 (terminal hypertrophy marker) in both FAA-CP and MCP groups (p = 0.030 and 0.014), and normoxia MCP had lower COL1A1 than hypoxia MCP (p = 0.013). Thus hypoxia limited terminal hypertrophy without improving core chondrogenic gene expression.

**Superior matrix synthesis by normoxia MCPs**
All groups showed trilineage potential with comparable Oil Red O, Alizarin Red, Picrosirius red, and collagen type II staining. Normoxia MCP pellets showed stronger Alcian blue and Toluidine blue uptake and significantly higher GAG/DNA than normoxia FAA-CP (p = 0.001), hypoxia FAA-CP (p = 0.002), and hypoxia MCP (p = 0.009)—corroborating the gene-expression findings.

---

## Perspective

This is the first head-to-head comparison of human FAA-CPs and MCPs derived from the same osteoarthritic cartilage source, and the first to examine hypoxia on migratory chondroprogenitor chondrogenesis, positioning MCPs as strong candidates for hyaline-like cartilage repair.

First, migratory chondroprogenitors under normoxia combined the strongest chondrogenic signature (high ACAN, highest COL2A1/COL1A1 ratio, greatest GAG/DNA) with the lowest fibrocartilage/hypertrophy markers (COL1A1, RUNX2)—the profile most desirable for producing hyaline rather than fibrocartilage repair tissue. Notably, MCPs achieved this without the exogenous growth factors (TGFβ2, FGF2) required for FAA-CP expansion, a practical advantage for scale-up.

Second, oxygen tension acted mainly on terminal hypertrophy: hypoxia lowered COL10A1 in both progenitor types without boosting chondrogenic genes, suggesting culture-oxygen tuning could be used to suppress endochondral/hypertrophic drift—relevant to avoiding the ectopic ossification and fibrocartilage that undermine current cartilage therapies.

Third, the shared MSC immunophenotype but divergent functional behavior underscores that surface-marker equivalence does not predict chondrogenic quality; CD146 and CD49b enrichment in MCPs may serve as practical selection markers.

Limitations include the small donor number (n = 3), an inflammatory OA source that may have influenced cell properties, and the use of subset-specific (non-identical) media that complicates direct RT-PCR comparison. Validation with non-diseased-joint cells, harmonized media, in-depth surfaceome/proteomic analysis, and in vivo repair models are the proposed next steps.
