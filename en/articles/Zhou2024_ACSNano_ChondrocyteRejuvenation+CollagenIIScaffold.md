# Rejuvenating Hyaline Cartilaginous Phenotype of Dedifferentiated Chondrocytes in Collagen II Scaffolds: A Mechanism Study Using Chondrocyte Membrane Nanoaggregates as Antagonists

## Citation (NLM)

Zhou H, Mu Y, Ma C, Zhang Z, Tao C, Wang DA. Rejuvenating Hyaline Cartilaginous Phenotype of Dedifferentiated Chondrocytes in Collagen II Scaffolds. ACS Nano. 2024;18(3):2077-2090. doi:10.1021/acsnano.3c09033

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1021/acsnano.3c09033](https://doi.org/10.1021/acsnano.3c09033)

---

## Background

Articular cartilage has extremely limited self-renewal capacity due to its avascular and aneural nature, and cartilage damage ultimately leads to osteoarthritis (OA). A type II collagen (Col2)-rich extracellular matrix (ECM) plays a critical role in maintaining the chondrocyte phenotype. However, during monolayer culture in vitro, chondrocytes undergo dedifferentiation toward a fibroblast-like phenotype, characterized by decreased Col2 expression and increased type I collagen (Col1) expression. Although Col2-based scaffolds have been reported to induce redifferentiation of chondrocytes, the underlying molecular mechanisms remained largely unknown. This study aimed to elucidate the mechanism of Col2 scaffold–chondrocyte interactions by using chondrocyte cell membrane (CCM) nanoaggregates as antagonists of Col2.

---

## Key Experiment Methods

**1. CCM nanoaggregate preparation and antCol2S fabrication**
- Isolated CCM vesicles from primary porcine chondrocytes (P0) by hypotonic lysis and centrifugation
- DLS size: 205.50 ± 2.20 nm, zeta potential: −22.83 ± 0.84 mV
- Prepared anti-Col2 scaffold (antCol2S) by immersing decellularized hyaline cartilage grafts (Col2S) in CCM suspension
- Confirmed successful coating by contact angle measurement, SEM, fluorescence microscopy, and proteomics

**2. Cell culture and dedifferentiation model**
- Induced dedifferentiation of porcine chondrocytes by monolayer culture from passage 1 (P1) to passage 5 (P5)
- Seeded P5 dedifferentiated chondrocytes onto Col2S and antCol2S at 8×10⁵ cells/scaffold density, cultured for 6 weeks in 3D

**3. Phenotype assessment**
- Phalloidin/DAPI staining for cytoskeletal observation
- qRT-PCR: COL2A1, ACAN, SOX9, COL1A1 gene expression
- Safranin-O staining (GAG), Col2/Col1 immunohistochemistry (IHC)
- GAG/DNA quantitative biochemical analysis
- Western blot: Col2, Col1, β-catenin, integrin α5 protein expression

**4. Transcriptomic and proteomic analysis**
- RNA-seq: comparison across P1, P5, Col2S, and antCol2S groups; GO/KEGG enrichment, GSEA
- Proteomics: label-free quantification by LC-MS/MS, DEP analysis

**5. Wnt signaling pathway analysis**
- Protein expression of Wnt signaling core components: β-catenin, GSK3β, APC
- ELISA quantification of sFRP3 (FRZB) secretion
- Correlation analysis between integrin α5 (ITGA5) expression and sFRP3 secretion

---

## Results

**Hyaline cartilage phenotype rejuvenation by Col2S**
After 6 weeks of culturing P5 dedifferentiated chondrocytes in Col2S, rounded chondrocyte morphology was restored, and COL2A1, ACAN, and SOX9 expression significantly increased (p<0.001). Safranin-O staining and Col2 IHC showed markedly increased GAG and Col2 deposition compared to controls, while COL1A1 was barely expressed. COL10A1 (hypertrophy marker) was not detected, confirming that Col2S restores a pure hyaline cartilage phenotype without hypertrophy.

**Redifferentiation effect abrogated by antCol2S**
In antCol2S, where Col2 ligands were saturated by CCM coating, COL2A1, ACAN, and SOX9 expression was significantly lower than in Col2S (p<0.01), while COL1A1 was increased. GAG content was also significantly lower than in Col2S (p<0.01). Cell morphology showed a mixture of fibroblast-like shapes, indicating partial failure of redifferentiation.

**Central role of the Wnt/β-catenin signaling pathway**
Transcriptomic and proteomic analyses showed that Col2S induced strong downregulation of Wnt/β-catenin signaling, while Wnt signaling was upregulated in antCol2S. GSEA revealed significant enrichment of the Wnt signaling pathway in both Col2S/P5 and Col2S/antCol2S comparisons. β-catenin protein was degraded in Col2S (low expression) but accumulated in P5 and antCol2S (high expression). In Col2S, Wnt negative regulators (WIF1, DKKL1, TLE2, APOE) were increased and positive regulators (DVL1, DVL2, FZD1, FZD6) were decreased.

**Mechanism of the integrin α5–sFRP3 axis**
In Col2S, integrin α5 (ITGA5) expression increased (activated binding to Col2 ligand), promoting secretion of the Wnt antagonist sFRP3 (FRZB). sFRP3 induced β-catenin degradation and activated SOX9 transcriptional activity, increasing Col2 expression. In antCol2S, CCM coating pre-occupied the Col2 ligand, blocking integrin α5 binding, leading to decreased sFRP3 secretion, Wnt pathway activation, SOX9 suppression, and reduced Col2 expression. ## Perspective

This study is the first mechanistic investigation to elucidate the molecular mechanism by which Col2-based scaffolds induce rejuvenation of the hyaline cartilage phenotype in dedifferentiated chondrocytes, with the following significance.

First, by employing the innovative antagonist strategy of CCM nanoaggregates, a novel approach to modulating cell–matrix interactions from the "scaffold perspective rather than the cell perspective" was presented. This avoids the risk of cytotoxicity associated with receptor blocking.

Second, the signaling pathway from Col2 ligand–integrin α5 receptor binding → sFRP3 secretion → Wnt/β-catenin signaling inhibition → SOX9 activation was systematically demonstrated. This provides a molecular rationale for the design of cartilage tissue engineering scaffolds.

Third, Col2S was shown to promote hyaline cartilage redifferentiation without inducing COL10A1 expression or hypertrophy, suggesting a low risk of calcification or hypertrophic failure in clinical applications.

Fourth, the reliability of label-free quantification (without iTRAQ/TMT dependence) was validated through integrated transcriptomic–proteomic analysis.

Study limitations include the use of porcine rather than human chondrocytes and the low yield of CCM preparation. Validation in human knee chondrocytes and development of large-scale production methods remain as future tasks. Nevertheless, the mechanism elucidated in this study provides an important molecular foundation for the clinical translation of Col2-based scaffold cartilage regeneration therapies.
