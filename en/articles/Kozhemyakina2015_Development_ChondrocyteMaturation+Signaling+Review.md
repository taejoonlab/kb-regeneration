# A pathway to bone: signaling molecules and transcription factors involved in chondrocyte development and maturation

## Citation (NLM)
Kozhemyakina E, Lassar AB, Zelzer E. A pathway to bone: signaling molecules and transcription factors involved in chondrocyte development and maturation. Development. 2015;142(5):817-831. doi:10.1242/dev.105536

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1242/dev.105536](https://doi.org/10.1242/dev.105536)

---

## Background

Most of the vertebrate skeleton is formed through endochondral ossification. In this process, mesenchymal progenitors differentiate into chondrocytes upon initiation of Sox9 expression, then undergo proliferation and hypertrophy in the growth plate before being replaced by bone. The growth plate serves as the "engine" of bone elongation, where chondrocytes sequentially progress through resting → columnar → prehypertrophic → early hypertrophic → late hypertrophic stages. Each stage is distinguished by the expression of specific markers (Col2a1, Ihh, Col10a1, VEGFA, Mmp13, etc.). Although signaling pathways and transcription factors regulating this differentiation process have been identified over decades, the integrated molecular mechanism remains incompletely understood.

---

## Key Experiment Methods

This paper is a **review article** and does not present primary experimental data. It synthesizes decades of accumulated in vitro and in vivo studies to organize the chondrocyte differentiation pathway. Major research approaches include:

- **Gene knockout/overexpression mouse models**: Growth plate phenotype analysis using conditional or systemic knockout/overexpression mice for Sox9, Runx2, Runx3, Mef2c, Foxa2, Hdac4, Fgfr3, Ihh, PTHrP, CNP, etc.
- **Micromass culture systems**: Chondrogenesis induction experiments using high-density culture of limb mesenchymal cells.
- **Cell morphology and cytoskeleton analysis**: Experiments investigating the relationship between RhoA signaling, spherical cell shape, and chondrogenic capacity.
- **Signaling pathway manipulation**: Functional analysis using ligands/receptors/inhibitors of BMP, Wnt, FGF, PTHrP, Ihh, CNP, TGFβ, RA, IGF1, etc.
- **Developmental genetics analysis**: Chicken embryo transplantation experiments, mouse conditional knockout (Cre-lox system), etc.

## Results

### Sox9 and initiation of chondrogenesis
- Sox9 is the key transcription factor for chondrogenesis, inducing Sox5 and Sox6 expression to activate chondrogenic differentiation markers including Col2a1 and Acan.
- **Shh**: Induces Sox9 expression in somitic mesenchymal cells, conferring chondrogenic capacity.
- **Wnt (β-catenin)**: Inhibits Sox9 expression, blocking chondrogenesis (FGF counters this inhibitory effect through DNMT3A phosphorylation).
- **BMP**: Essential for chondrogenesis initiation and maintenance; cartilage formation lacking in Bmpr1a/1b double knockout mice.
- **PKA**: Enhances transcriptional activity through Sox9 phosphorylation.
- **RA (retinoic acid)**: Suppresses Sox9 expression through activation of Wnt signaling.
- **Hif1α**: Promotes Sox9 expression and VEGFA expression in the hypoxic environment of early cartilage formation.

### Regulation of chondrocyte hypertrophy
- **Runx2/Runx3**: Positively regulate hypertrophic chondrocyte differentiation; double knockout results in complete absence of hypertrophy.
- **Mef2c/Mef2d**: Promote hypertrophy and act upstream of Runx2.
- **FoxA2/FoxA3**: Hypertrophic chondrocyte-specific cofactors required for Col10a1 and Mmp13 expression.
- **Hdac4**: Negatively regulates hypertrophy by inhibiting Runx2 and Mef2 activity; nuclear translocation regulated by PTHrP-PKA-PP2A signaling.

### PTHrP-Ihh signaling loop
- **Ihh**: Expressed in prehypertrophic chondrocytes, inducing PTHrP synthesis in periarticular chondrocytes.
- **PTHrP**: Inhibits chondrocyte hypertrophy and maintains proliferation; acts through Pth1r → PKA → Hdac4 nuclear translocation → Mef2/Runx2 inhibition pathway.
- This feedback loop maintains the immature chondrocyte pool.

### FGF signaling
- **Fgfr3**: Negatively regulates chondrocyte proliferation and hypertrophy initiation in the proliferative zone; human gain-of-function mutations cause achondroplasia.
- Fgf18/Fgfr3 signaling has dual roles depending on developmental stage: promotes proliferation and hypertrophy at E14-15, inhibits them thereafter.
- Stat1 pathway → proliferation inhibition; MAPK pathway → inhibition of hypertrophy initiation.

### CNP signaling
- CNP/Npr2 signaling antagonizes the Fgf18/Fgfr3 MAPK pathway to maintain an appropriate rate of hypertrophy.
- CNP overexpression rescues the dwarfism phenotype of Fgfr3 gain-of-function mice.

### Terminal chondrocyte hypertrophy
- **EGFR (EGF/TGFα)**: Inhibits Col10a1 expression and induces Mmp9, Mmp13, and RANKL expression, promoting terminal hypertrophic conversion.
- **ROS**: Specifically generated in the growth plate hypertrophic zone, inducing Mmp13 expression.
- **IGF1**: Controls the final cell volume increase stage of hypertrophic chondrocytes.

### Final fate of chondrocytes
- Some Col10a1+ hypertrophic chondrocytes transdifferentiate into osteocytes instead of undergoing apoptosis (lineage tracing study by Yang et al., 2014).

---

## Perspective

- This review provides an integrated picture of signaling pathways and transcription factor networks regulating chondrocyte differentiation.
- Further identification of regulatory elements controlling Sox9 expression is needed, which will contribute to understanding the spatial and temporal patterning of skeletal development.
- Understanding these molecular mechanisms could lead to new treatments for various skeletal disorders such as achondroplasia and chondrodysplasia.
- Whether hypertrophic chondrocytes undergo transdifferentiation into osteocytes and the regulatory mechanisms involved remain important future research topics.
