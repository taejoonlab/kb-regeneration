# RUNX transcription factors: orchestrators of development

## Citation (NLM)
Mevel R, Draper JE, Lie-a-Ling M, Kouskoff V, Lacaud G. RUNX transcription factors: orchestrators of development. Development. 2019;146(19):dev148296. doi:10.1242/dev.148296

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1242/dev.148296](https://doi.org/10.1242/dev.148296)

---

## Background
The RUNX family of transcription factors (RUNX1, RUNX2, RUNX3) orchestrates various developmental and cellular processes, including cell proliferation, differentiation, and lineage specification. This primer introduces the molecular hallmarks of the three mammalian RUNX genes, discusses the regulation of their activities and mechanisms of action, and reviews their crucial roles in the specification and maintenance of a wide array of tissues during embryonic development and adult homeostasis.

## Key Experiment Methods
1. RUNX knockout mouse model analysis (global knockout, conditional knockout)
2. RUNX gene structure and promoter (P1/P2) analysis, alternative splicing studies
3. Runt homology domain (RHD) DNA binding and protein-protein interaction analysis
4. Post-translational modification analysis (acetylation, phosphorylation, methylation, ubiquitylation, SUMOylation)
5. Dimerization with CBFβ and co-regulator interaction studies
6. Lineage-specific Cre driver conditional knockouts (Vav1-Cre, Col2a1-Cre, Lck-Cre, etc.)
7. Double knockout (DKO) models for functional redundancy analysis

## Results
- RUNX gene architecture: all three genes contain two promoters (distal P1, proximal P2) and generate diverse isoforms via alternative splicing. RHD mediates DNA binding (PyGPyGGTPy), protein interactions, and nuclear localization
- RUNX1: master regulator of hematopoiesis. Essential for endothelial-to-hematopoietic transition (EHT); global knockout causes embryonic lethality (E12.5). Partially dispensable in adult hematopoiesis, but deletion leads to HSPC expansion, reduced megakaryocytes/platelets, and decreased B/T cells
- RUNX2: master regulator of skeletal development. Global knockout causes neonatal lethality (respiratory failure), complete absence of bone ossification, entirely cartilaginous skeleton. Essential for osteoblast differentiation (regulates Osterix/Sp7) and promotes chondrocyte maturation
- RUNX3: important for neurogenesis and immune cells. Global knockout causes gastric epithelial hyperplasia, T cell defects, motor dysfunction. Partially redundant with RUNX2: Runx2/3 DKO shows complete absence of chondrocyte differentiation
- RUNX2/3 cooperate in chondrocyte development: RUNX2 promotes chondrocyte maturation and regulates hypertrophy; RUNX3 regulates chondrocyte proliferation. In perichondrium, RUNX2 inhibits chondrocyte proliferation/hypertrophy
- Post-translational modifications: acetylation increases activity, HDAC association represses, phosphorylation has context-dependent effects, ubiquitylation regulates degradation

## Perspective
RUNX transcription factors play essential roles across hematopoietic, skeletal, nervous, and immune lineages, with spatiotemporal expression patterns explaining their non-redundant functions. Small-molecule inhibitors of RUNX/CBFβ complexes show therapeutic potential in cancer. Single-cell transcriptomics is expected to further reveal co-expression patterns and context-specific functions. From a cartilage regeneration perspective, the mechanisms by which RUNX2/3 regulate chondrocyte differentiation and maturation provide important insights for cartilage engineering.
