# Frizzled 1 and Wnt1 as new potential therapeutic targets in the traumatically injured spinal cord

## Citation (NLM)
González P, González-Fernández C, Campos-Martín Y, Mollejo M, Carballosa-Gautam M, Marcillo A, Norenberg M, Rodríguez FJ. Frizzled 1 and Wnt1 as new potential therapeutic targets in the traumatically injured spinal cord. Cell Mol Life Sci. 2020;77(23):4631-4662. doi:10.1007/s00018-019-03427-4

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1007/s00018-019-03427-4](https://doi.org/10.1007/s00018-019-03427-4)

---

## Background

The Wnt family of secreted glycoproteins (19 ligands, conventional Frizzled receptors Fz1–10, non-conventional receptors Ror1/2, Ryk, PTK7, co-receptors LRP5/6, and soluble modulators) plays essential roles in CNS development and adult homeostasis, and its components are dysregulated during spinal cord injury (SCI). While rodent studies implicate Wnt signaling in axonal regeneration, glial reactivity, scar formation, myelin preservation, and neural precursor differentiation after SCI, its relevance in the healthy and injured *human* spinal cord and its therapeutic potential remain poorly understood.

Prior work by the authors showed the Fz1 receptor is expressed in the adult CNS, changes in various neuropathologies, and mediates neuroprotection by its ligand Wnt1 in models of Parkinson's disease, cerebral ischemia, and subarachnoid hemorrhage. This study aimed to (1) characterize broad Wnt-family expression in the healthy human spinal cord, (2) define the spatio-temporal/cellular expression of Fz1 in injured human and rat spinal cord, and (3) test the therapeutic potential of lentiviral Wnt1 overexpression in a clinically relevant rat contusion SCI model.

---

## Key Experiment Methods

1. **Human spinal cord samples** — Non-lesioned (control) and traumatically lesioned (1–5 days post-injury) donor tissue, fresh-frozen and paraffin-embedded, from biobank and University of Miami sources.
2. **qRT-PCR** — mRNA expression of Wnt ligands, receptors, co-receptors, and soluble modulators in healthy human spinal cord using customized TaqMan Low Density Arrays.
3. **Immunohistochemistry** — Chromogen-based simple IHC for all conventional/non-conventional Wnt receptors (Fz1–10, Ror1/2, Ryk, PTK7, pLRP6) in human tissue; fluorescence-based double IHC to define Fz1 cellular expression (astrocytes, microglia, neurons, oligodendrocytes, axons, blood vessels) in human and rat spinal cord.
4. **Rat contusive SCI model** — 87 adult female Wistar rats; spinal cord contusion; Fz1 expression analyzed at 6 h to 28 days post-injury.
5. **Lentiviral overexpression** — Self-inactivating lentivirus (LV-GFP control vs. LV-Wnt1) intraparenchymally injected after contusion; vector validated by flow cytometry, qRT-PCR, and Western blot (Wnt1 and active β-catenin).
6. **Outcome measures** — BBB open-field motor scoring and CatWalk gait analysis; eriochrome cyanine myelin staining; densitometry of GFAP, Iba1, NG2, and 5-HT axons; automated cell counts of NeuN+ neurons and APC+ oligodendrocytes.

---

## Results

- **Broad Wnt expression in healthy human spinal cord:** Detectable mRNA of most Wnt ligands (Wnt2, 2b, 3, 4, 5a, 5b, 6, 7a, 7b, 8b, 9b, 10a, 10b, 11, 16), all receptors (Fz1–10, Ror1/2, PTK7, Ryk), co-receptors (LRP5/6), and soluble modulators (Dkk1–4, sFRP1–5, Wif1); Wnt1, 3a, 8a, 9a were undetectable. All Wnt receptors and phosphorylated LRP6 were confirmed at the protein level with distinct expression patterns.
- **Fz1 in human spinal cord:** Expressed in white matter astrocytes near the pial surface, quiescent microglia, axons, blood vessels, neurons, and oligodendrocytes. After injury, Fz1-expressing reactive astrocytes increased around the lesion (most evident at 5 dpi), with expression also in activated microglia/macrophages, axons, and blood vessels.
- **Fz1 in rat SCI is equivalent to human:** Similar baseline cellular expression; after contusion, dynamic changes including increased Fz1+ astrocytes and glial-scar NG2+ cells, loss of endothelial expression, and near-disappearance in activated amoeboid microglia — closely mirroring the human pattern, validating the rat model.
- **Therapeutic benefit of Wnt1 overexpression:** Lentiviral LV-Wnt1 was validated to overexpress functional, secreted Wnt1 that activated β-catenin. Per the abstract, long-term Wnt1 overexpression after rat SCI improved motor functional recovery, increased myelin preservation and neuronal survival, and reduced early astroglial reactivity and NG2+ cell accumulation.

---

## Perspective

This is the first comprehensive characterization of Wnt-family expression in the healthy and traumatically injured human spinal cord, establishing translational relevance for a signaling system previously studied mostly in rodents. The demonstration that Fz1 expression patterns are conserved between human and rat SCI validates the rat contusion model for Wnt-targeted therapeutic development, and the beneficial effects of Wnt1 overexpression (neuroprotection, myelin preservation, reduced glial reactivity, improved locomotion) nominate the Wnt1/Fz1 axis as a candidate therapeutic target for SCI.

Limitations include the small number of human lesioned samples (n=6, wide age range, single early time points up to 5 days), the descriptive/correlative nature of the human data, and reliance on lentiviral overexpression rather than a clinically deliverable agent. Future work should clarify the receptor-specific mechanism (Fz1 dependence in the spinal cord), test pharmacological Wnt1 pathway activation, and evaluate long-term safety given Wnt signaling's roles in proliferation and tumorigenesis.
