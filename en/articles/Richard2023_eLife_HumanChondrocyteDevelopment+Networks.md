# Lineage-specific differences and regulatory networks governing human chondrocyte development

## Citation (NLM)
Richard D, Pregizer S, Venkatasubramanian D, Raftery RM, Muthuirulan P, Liu Z, Capellini TD, Craft AM. Lineage-specific differences and regulatory networks governing human chondrocyte development. eLife. 2023;12:e79925. doi:10.7554/eLife.79925

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.7554/eLife.79925](https://doi.org/10.7554/eLife.79925)

---

## Background
Articular cartilage and growth plate cartilage represent two distinct chondrogenic lineages with critical but different roles in the musculoskeletal system. Articular cartilage provides frictionless joint movement, while growth plate cartilage serves as a template for endochondral bone elongation. Despite extensive research in mouse models, significant gaps remain in understanding the molecular regulation of human chondrocyte development, particularly the gene regulatory networks that specify and maintain these distinct lineages. This knowledge gap limits development of pharmaceutical or gene-based treatments for skeletal dysplasias and osteoarthritis.

## Key Experiment Methods
1. Directed differentiation of human embryonic stem cells (hESCs) into articular-like cartilage (TGFβ3 treatment) and growth plate-like cartilage (BMP4 treatment) using a 12-week micromass culture protocol
2. Bulk RNA-sequencing of hESC-derived articular and growth plate cartilage tissues, compared with fetal epiphyseal and growth plate chondrocytes isolated from E67 fetal donor tissue
3. ATAC-sequencing to map chromatin accessibility in hESC-derived chondrocyte lineages and mouse embryonic chondrocytes (Col2a1+ and Col10a1+ sorted cells from E15.5 transgenic mice)
4. Integration of transcriptomic and epigenetic datasets to define lineage-specific gene regulatory networks using three metrics: gene expression, promoter accessibility, and cis-regulatory score (enhancer activity)
5. Classification of genes into four regulatory behavior clusters: unexplained, combo-centric, promoter-centric, and enhancer-centric
6. Validation of differential gene expression by qPCR across multiple independent differentiations and fetal developmental timepoints (E59, E67, E72)
7. In situ hybridization (RNAscope) and immunohistochemistry to localize gene and protein expression in cartilage tissues
8. Motif enrichment analysis to identify transcription factor binding patterns in differentially accessible regions

## Results
- hESC-derived articular and growth plate chondrocytes showed distinct transcriptional profiles that closely recapitulated fetal cartilage counterparts; >70% of top 200 differentially expressed genes (DEGs) showed consistent directionality between in vitro and in vivo samples
- Articular cartilage was enriched for ECM organization, TGF stimulus response, and collagen processes; growth plate cartilage was enriched for ossification and cartilage development
- Novel lineage-specific markers identified: CHI3L1 (YKL-40) and MEOX1 enriched in articular cartilage; EFHD1 (mitochondrial calcium-binding protein) specifically expressed in hypertrophic growth plate chondrocytes
- COL15A1 protein localized to superficial zone matrix of articular cartilage, suggesting zone-specific expression
- ATAC-seq revealed 37,780 unique peaks; 12,154 regions more accessible in growth plate and 11,571 in articular chondrocytes
- A simplistic model of TF expression correlating with target accessibility was insufficient; many motifs enriched in DARs were not for differentially expressed TFs
- Integration of RNA-seq and ATAC-seq identified lineage-specific regulatory networks; RUNX2 validated as key TF in growth plate chondrocytes, RELA validated as key TF in articular chondrocytes
- Timecourse analysis (4, 8, 12 weeks) showed progressive lineage divergence with high reproducibility across independent differentiations

## Perspective
This study provides a comprehensive resource of transcriptomic and epigenetic profiles for two major human chondrocyte lineages, bridging the gap between mouse models and human cartilage biology. The hESC-based differentiation platform offers a scalable system for studying human chondrogenesis that closely mirrors fetal development. The finding that gene regulatory behavior falls into distinct clusters (promoter-centric, enhancer-centric, combo-centric) reveals complexity beyond simple TF-target relationships. Validation of RUNX2 and RELA as lineage-specific regulators provides concrete targets for further functional studies. These datasets will facilitate understanding of skeletal dysplasias, osteoarthritis pathogenesis, and development of targeted therapies for cartilage repair.
