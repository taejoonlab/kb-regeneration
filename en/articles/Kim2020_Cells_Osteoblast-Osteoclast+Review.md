# Osteoblast-Osteoclast Communication and Bone Homeostasis

## Citation (NLM)
Kim JM, Lin C, Stavre Z, Greenblatt MB, Shim JH. Osteoblast-Osteoclast Communication and Bone Homeostasis. Cells. 2020;9(9):2073. doi:10.3390/cells9092073

**DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.3390/cells9092073](https://doi.org/10.3390/cells9092073)

---

## Background
Bone is a dynamic tissue that remodels continuously throughout life, providing mechanical support for stature and locomotion and protecting vital organs such as bone marrow and the brain. Bone remodeling is a process in which old or damaged bone is removed by osteoclasts and replaced with new bone formed by osteoblasts. The balance between osteoblast-mediated bone formation and osteoclast-mediated bone resorption is tightly regulated. Dysregulation of this balance results in abnormal bone remodeling, leading to postmenopausal and secondary forms of osteoporosis. This review illustrates the key mediators that control the cross-talk between osteoblasts and osteoclasts through cell–cell contact or secretory factors.

## Key Experiment Methods
1. EPHB4 overexpressing transgenic mice for bone mass analysis
2. Osteoblast-specific FASL conditional knockout mouse models
3. Tnfrsf11a (RANK) and Tnfsf11 (RANKL) deficient mice (osteopetrosis models)
4. Tnfrsf11b (OPG) deficient mice (osteoporosis models)
5. Osteoblast- or osteoclast-specific Wnt5a/Ror2 knockout mice
6. Sema4d-deficient and Plxnb1-deficient mouse models
7. Osteoclast-specific Ctsk knockout mice (increased S1P production model)
8. Clinical and preclinical studies of Denosumab (anti-RANKL antibody), odanacatib (CTSK inhibitor), and PTH (anabolic agent)

## Results
1. EFNB2-EPHB4 interaction mediates bidirectional signaling; reverse signaling blocks the C-FOS/NFATC1 cascade to suppress osteoclastogenesis, while forward signaling promotes osteoblast differentiation and suppresses apoptosis
2. Estrogen upregulates FASL expression in osteoblasts to induce pre-osteoclast apoptosis; FASL deficiency increases osteoclast number and activity, reducing bone mass
3. SEMA3A binds NRP1 to inhibit RANKL-induced osteoclastogenesis and promotes osteoblast differentiation via the WNT/β-catenin pathway
4. RANKL-RANK signaling is essential for osteoclast differentiation, fusion, and activation; OPG acts as a decoy receptor for RANKL to inhibit osteoclastogenesis
5. WNT5A binds ROR2 to activate the JNK MAPK pathway and upregulates RANK expression in osteoclasts, promoting osteoclastogenesis
6. WNT16 is highly expressed in cortical bone and directly inhibits osteoclastogenesis while indirectly suppressing it by upregulating OPG expression in osteoblasts
7. Osteoclast-derived S1P promotes osteoblast migration and survival; Ctsk deficiency increases S1P production, enhancing bone formation
8. Osteoclast-derived SEMA4D binds PLXNB1 to activate RHOA and attenuate IGF-1 signaling, suppressing osteoblast differentiation
9. Osteoclast-derived CTHRC1 recruits stromal cells and induces osteoblast differentiation; C3 is cleaved to C3a to promote osteoblastogenesis

## Perspective
Osteoblast-osteoclast communication is essential for maintaining bone homeostasis, and understanding these interactions is critical for developing therapeutics for osteoporosis and other bone diseases. Currently approved agents (Denosumab, odanacatib, PTH, etc.) all affect osteoblast-osteoclast interactions, with anti-resorptive agents simultaneously suppressing bone formation and anabolic agents increasing bone resorption. Odanacatib, which inhibits CTSK to suppress osteoclast activity with modest effects on bone formation, showed promise but was discontinued due to cerebrovascular event risks. Future research should focus on deeper understanding of the reversal phase, elucidating spatial and temporal regulation of bone remodeling in pathologic bone loss contexts, and identifying novel factors regulating osteoblast/osteoclast communication.
